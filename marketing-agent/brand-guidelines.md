# NounCompass Brand Guidelines

## Brand position

NounCompass is an independent student-help platform for National Open University of Nigeria students and prospective students.

It helps students understand processes, find useful guides, and get support without pretending to be NOUN itself.

Always keep this disclaimer ready for captions, bios, landing pages, and replies where confusion is possible:

NounCompass is an independent student-help platform and is not the official NOUN website.

## Non-negotiables

- Do not present NounCompass as the official NOUN website.
- Do not imply school authority, insider access, or guaranteed outcomes.
- Do not use fake urgency, fake deadlines, or pressure tactics.
- Do not say "guaranteed admission", "official slot", or similar claims.
- Do not ask users to share sensitive personal data in public comments.
- Do not use copyrighted NOUN logos unless explicit permission is provided.

## Brand voice

- Helpful
- Clear
- Student-friendly
- Trustworthy
- Simple
- Confident

## Audience segments

- New applicants who need admission clarity
- Returning students trying to solve fees or registration issues
- Students stuck in the portal
- Students searching for course materials
- Students preparing for TMA or exams
- Students checking results or academic progress
- Students who want a human guide before they make a mistake

## Lead CTAs

- Need help with NOUN admission? Contact NounCompass for guidance.
- Need help understanding your next NOUN step? Read the guide on NounCompass.
- Still confused after reading? Contact NounCompass for guidance.
- Save this post and check the full guide on NounCompass.

## Content rules

- Lead with a real student problem, not a brand announcement.
- Give one clear next step.
- Link each post to the best matching page, article, or contact path.
- Use the disclaimer when the caption, creative, or reply could be mistaken for official NOUN communication.
- Keep visuals readable on mobile with short headlines.
- Make posts useful even if the viewer never becomes a lead.
- Avoid cluttered designs and long text overlays.
- Default social creatives should look like premium branded flyers, not plain quote cards.
- Every generated flyer should carry the NounCompass logo plus clear contact details such as `nouncompass.me` and `support@nouncompass.me` when space allows.
- Visuals should feel polished, premium, and trust-building enough to make students want to visit the website or contact NounCompass.
- Strong lead-focused posts can be published directly once prepared unless a login, 2FA, payment, or account risk step requires manual intervention.

## Best-performing content angles for now

- Safe fee payment and Remita guidance
- Admission clarity for confused applicants
- Portal, password, and registration troubleshooting
- TMA and exam mistake prevention
- Course materials discovery and study help
- Results, CGPA, and outstanding-course explanation

## Safety and compliance checklist

- Is this misleading?
- Does this sound official when it is not?
- Does this make a false promise?
- Does this violate platform rules?
- Does this risk AdSense approval?
- Does this expose private student data?
- Is the CTA clear?
- Is the post useful even if the user does not buy anything?
