## Quick Answer

Maximum credit units per semester varies by program but typically:
- **Undergraduate:** 18–24 credit units per semester
- **Postgraduate:** 12–18 credit units per semester

This translates to roughly:
- **Undergraduate:** 4–6 courses
- **Postgraduate:** 3–5 courses

Check your program requirements in NOUN portal for exact maximum.

---


## What This Guide Covers

This guide explains how credit units affect registration, how to think about carryovers and electives, and why students must verify limits before submission.


## Understanding Credit Units

Each course carries a credit unit value:
- **3-unit course:** Most common
- **2-unit course:** Less common
- **4-unit course:** Major courses
- **1-unit course:** Rare

To calculate your load:
- Register 6 courses × 3 units each = 18 credit units

---

## Why Maximum Limits Exist

- Prevents overloading students
- Ensures quality learning (not quantity)
- Meets accreditation standards
- Allows adequate study time

---

## How to Check Your Maximum

1. Log into NOUN portal
2. Go to "Course Registration"
3. Look for note saying "Maximum credit units allowed: XX"
4. Or contact your department

---

## Strategies for Course Planning

**If you want to finish faster:**
Register maximum allowed credit units each semester

**If you want to balance work/study:**
Register 12–15 credit units (fewer courses)

**If you're struggling:**
Drop to minimum (usually 12 units for undergrad)

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can I exceed the maximum?

Rarely. You need department permission to overload (register more than maximum).

### Q2: What if I need to take more courses?

Either take extra semester or request to overload (if eligible).

### Q3: Do more credit units mean higher fees?

Usually yes—more courses = higher fees.

---

## Important Warnings

⚠️ **Do NOT:**
- Exceed credit unit limit without explicit department permission
- Assume you can "handle" extra courses (overload causes failures)
- Register extra courses hoping to drop later (you might not be able to)
- Ignore the maximum limit—portal will enforce it

⚠️ **Critical:**
- Overloading without permission can result in course removal
- Working students should NEVER overload (workload + job = failure risk)
- Health issues combined with overload can derail your semester

✓ **DO:**
- Know your program's credit unit limits
- Request permission BEFORE registering beyond limit
- Be honest about your time availability
- Start with lighter loads if you're working or returning student

---

## Final Advice

The credit unit limit exists for your protection, not as a barrier. It's based on how much coursework typically leads to success.

Registering for more than the limit is a form of self-sabotage—it rarely results in successful completion. Even if you passed overloaded semesters in other universities, NOUN's pace is different. Respect the limits.

Focus on strong performance in regular loads rather than rushing through excessive ones. Quality passes are better than quantity failures.
