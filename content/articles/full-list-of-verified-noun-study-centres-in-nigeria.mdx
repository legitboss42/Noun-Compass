## Quick Answer

As of the 19 June 2026 verification pass, NounCompass was able to treat **31 NOUN study centres as verified**, **37 as partially verified**, and **34 as unverified**. This page lists only the centres that cleared the verified threshold, then shows where students should still be cautious before travelling.

If you only need the safest workflow, use this order:

1. start with the verified list on this page
2. use the city or state guide that matches your location
3. confirm the current centre detail through official NOUN pages
4. contact the centre or official support before travelling for a sensitive issue

## What "Verified" Means On This Page

This page does **not** copy the entire third-party directory as fact. A centre appears here only when the Phase 7.3 verification report found direct or strong official NOUN support, such as:

- a direct centre page on `nou.edu.ng`
- a strong official NOUN search result naming the centre
- an official NOUN announcement closely tied to the centre

If a row had only a weak signal, a partial naming match, or only a third-party listing, it was not promoted to the verified list below.

That distinction matters. Many students search for a "full list" and land on recycled directories. This page is meant to be narrower and more trustworthy.

## How Students Usually Use A Study Centre

Students search for study centres for practical reasons, not just curiosity. A study centre can matter when you need to:

- complete or follow up on admission screening
- clarify registration issues
- ask about result or academic-record problems
- understand local exam or student-support instructions
- confirm that a centre is still active before travelling

That does **not** mean every verified centre handles every task in the same way. Use the list as your starting point, then confirm the exact service you need through the centre or an official support route. If your issue is results-related, also use [How To Check NOUN Results](/articles/how-to-check-noun-results) and [How To Use NOUN Support Or e-Ticketing For Result Problems](/articles/how-to-use-noun-support-or-e-ticketing-for-result-problems).

## Common Mistakes Students Make

- treating a third-party directory as final without cross-checking
- assuming a partially verified centre is as safe to use as a verified one
- travelling for a result or registration issue without confirming the right service channel first
- copying a contact number from social media instead of an official source
- assuming a city must have a centre detail page just because many people search for it
