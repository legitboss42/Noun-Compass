## Where Is The TMA Deadline Shown?

The observed NOUN eLearn TMA pages displayed deadlines directly on the activity page through two fields: `Opened` and `Closed`. The safest way to avoid deadline mistakes is to open the exact TMA activity itself, read those timestamps carefully, and stop relying on memory, copied screenshots, or another student's timetable.

## The Most Important Deadline Lesson

A TMA deadline is not something you should infer from the dashboard alone. In the observed session, the most trustworthy timing surface was the TMA activity page itself.

That page showed:

- `Opened`
- `Closed`
- the instruction `Click on Attempt TMA Now to begin the TMA`
- `Attempts allowed: 1`

So the right question is not only `When is the deadline?`

It is also:

`Am I reading the deadline from the exact TMA activity page that belongs to my own course?`

## What We Observed In The Live Session

The reviewed course was `Numerical Analysis (MTH213_261)`. On the observed `TMA1` page, the activity header displayed:

- Opened: `Monday, 2 March 2026, 12:00 AM`
- Closed: `Sunday, 7 June 2026, 11:59 PM`

On the observed `TMA3` page, the pattern was the same, with a different open date.

That matters because the platform did not hide timing in a secondary menu. It placed the deadline information directly in the TMA header.

## Where Students Should Check TMA Deadlines

Based on the live session, check in this order:

1. the exact TMA activity page
2. the course page to confirm you chose the right TMA
3. the dashboard if you need a quick navigation route, not a final timing decision

The reason for this order is simple:

- the dashboard helps you find the task
- the course page helps you verify the right TMA
- the TMA activity page tells you the actual timing

## How To Read The Deadline Safely

### Step 1: Open the exact TMA

Use the dashboard, the course page, or the TMA course index to open the correct assignment. If you need help with that first step, read [How To Find TMA On NOUN eLearn](/articles/how-to-find-tma-on-noun-elearn).

### Step 2: Read `Opened`

This field tells you when the activity became available. It helps you verify whether you are looking at a live task or reviewing an old one.

### Step 3: Read `Closed`

This is the practical deadline field. Do not skip it just because you already know roughly when the course wants submissions.

### Step 4: Check `Attempts allowed`

A deadline matters even more when the activity also shows `Attempts allowed: 1`, as the observed TMA pages did.

### Step 5: Re-check before you act

If you left the page and came back later, read the timing again. Deadline mistakes often happen when students rely on what they think they saw yesterday.

## Why Students Miss TMA Deadlines

The observed interface makes certain mistakes more likely:

- students may find the TMA link through `Recently accessed items` and click too quickly
- the long course page can make students feel they have already "found everything" before reaching the TMA block
- the phrase `Attempt TMA Now` can grab attention faster than the actual timing fields
- students may assume all three TMAs in a course close the same way without checking each page

This is why deadline checking should be deliberate, not emotional.

## Deadline-Safe Workflow

Use this practical pattern:

1. confirm the course and TMA label
2. open the TMA activity page
3. read `Opened`
4. read `Closed`
5. confirm `Attempts allowed`
6. only then decide whether you are ready to proceed
7. after submission, check the finished-state summary

This workflow connects directly to [How To Submit TMA On NOUN eLearn](/articles/how-to-submit-tma-on-noun-elearn) and the broader [NOUN eLearn and TMA guide](/articles/noun-elearn-and-tma-guide).

## Common Deadline Mistakes

- checking a dashboard shortcut but never reading the TMA header
- copying another student's deadline instead of reading your own activity page
- assuming all TMAs for a course follow the same visible timing without opening each one
- focusing on the top instruction line and ignoring the timing fields nearby
- starting a one-attempt TMA without enough time or preparation

If you want the wider prevention checklist, use [Common TMA Mistakes Students Make](/articles/common-noun-tma-mistakes).

## Troubleshooting

### The TMA looks closed already

Do not guess. Confirm the exact TMA page, read the `Closed` field again, and preserve a safe screenshot if the status seems inconsistent with an official announcement.

### I can find the course but not the deadline

Move from the course page into the TMA activity itself. The course page helps with navigation, but the activity page is where the timing was shown most clearly in the observed session.

### I am not sure whether I opened the right TMA

Return to the course page and verify the label against `TMA1`, `TMA2`, or `TMA3`.

### I missed the deadline and need help

Prepare the course code, the exact TMA label, and a safe screenshot of the page state before using the official support route.

## A Better Deadline Planning Routine

The observed platform suggests a simple routine students can repeat for every TMA:

1. open the exact TMA page the same day you first notice it
2. record the visible `Opened` and `Closed` fields in your own notes
3. return to the page again before the work session starts
4. re-check the fields instead of trusting memory
5. only then proceed through the submission workflow

This routine works because it is based on the actual page structure, not on assumptions about announcements or group chats. It also fits naturally with the observed one-attempt rule.

It also works better when you pair the TMA page with the course page itself. The course page helps you confirm that you opened the right assignment, while the TMA activity page tells you exactly when that assignment opens and closes.

## Verification Notes

This guide is grounded in a live eLearn session reviewed on 19 June 2026. It verifies the presence of `Opened` and `Closed` fields on real TMA activity pages. It does not claim that the specific observed dates apply to other courses. Use the pattern, not another student's timestamp.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Where is the real TMA deadline shown?

On the TMA activity page itself, through the `Opened` and `Closed` fields.

### Should I trust the dashboard for the final deadline?

No. The dashboard is useful for navigation. The TMA activity page is better for final timing decisions.

### Do I need to open every TMA separately?

Yes, if you want to verify timing safely. Do not assume one TMA's dates explain every other TMA in the course.

### Why does the attempt rule matter for deadlines?

Because the observed TMA pages showed `Attempts allowed: 1`, which makes timing mistakes more costly.

### What should I do if the deadline state still looks wrong?

Capture safe evidence and escalate through official support rather than relying on memory or copied screenshots.
