## Before you register for exams

Complete your course registration first, fund the required payment path, open the current official exam-registration workflow, choose only the courses you actually intend to sit, and save the final exam record immediately. If an expected course is missing, stop and verify it before paying.

1. Confirm that the course was already registered.
2. Open the current official exam-registration route.
3. Select the correct level and semester.
4. Tick the examinable courses you intend to sit.
5. Submit the registration and save the final exam record.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide explains how exam registration differs from course registration, how to verify the right courses before payment, how to keep the final record, and how to respond when a course or timetable detail does not look right.

## Requirements Before You Start

Keep the course-registration slip, current exam list, session, and payment reference together before submitting. Save the exam record privately and never expose portal or payment credentials in a support screenshot.

Keep these records nearby before you start:

- your course-registration slip
- the correct level and semester
- current fee or wallet readiness
- any timetable or examination notice tied to the same session
- a safe place to save the final exam-registration record

## The Golden Rule: Course Registration and Exam Registration Are Different Tasks

Registering a course does not automatically register the exam for that course. Students who confuse the two workflows can finish one payment, assume everything is complete, and only discover the problem close to the exam date. Treat the course record and the exam record as separate checkpoints.

## Step-by-Step Guide: NOUN Exam Registration

### Step 1: Confirm that the course is already on your record

Before you attempt exam registration, compare the course list with your saved course-registration slip. If the course was never registered properly, the exam workflow may not show it.

### Step 2: Open the current official exam-registration route

Log into the official student portal and open the exam-registration section from the student menu. Work only from the current official route because deadlines and available options can be sensitive.

### Step 3: Select the correct level and semester

Choose the correct level and semester before you interpret a missing course. Carryovers and lower-level courses may require extra attention so you do not search in the wrong place.

### Step 4: Review the displayed course list carefully

Tick only the courses you genuinely intend to sit. Compare the displayed list with your course-registration record and any current exam plan before submitting.

### Step 5: Review the charges and submit once

Check the displayed total, then submit the registration only when the selection is correct. Avoid repeating the process because of a slow page response until you confirm whether the first attempt actually completed.

### Step 6: Save the final exam record

Download, print, or safely capture the final exam-registration record as soon as it becomes available. This record can matter during clearance, timetable checks, attendance follow-up, or later result issues.

## Physical Clearance and Exam-Day Preparation

If your study centre or current workflow requires additional physical clearance, handle it before the exam period becomes crowded. Keep the exam-registration record, course-registration record, and any required identification or payment evidence together in one place.

## Understanding Exam Types and Timetable Risk

The same portal workflow can support different exam formats or centre arrangements. That is why you should confirm the current timetable, mode, venue, and date from authorised sources instead of assuming the arrangement from another student's message or a past semester screenshot.

If you are trying to understand the practical difference between exam formats before registration or preparation, use [NOUN E-Exam vs Pen-on-Paper (POP) Exam](/articles/noun-e-exam-vs-pop) as the next comparison guide.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Assuming course registration already covers exam registration.
- Looking for a missing course under the wrong level or semester.
- Registering in a rush without comparing the list to the saved course record.
- Waiting until the final days of the window before checking whether the workflow still opens properly.
- Failing to save the final exam-registration record after submission.

## When The Exam List Does Not Match

### A registered course does not appear for examination

Compare course and exam records, confirm the course is examinable under the current instructions, and document the missing entry. Do not wait until examination day to seek clarification.

### An unexpected course appears

Do not submit automatically. Verify the code, title, programme relevance, and registration history. Report an unexplained entry through the authorised channel before relying on the record.

### The timetable conflicts with an informal message

Give priority to the current official timetable or authorised update. Check the publication date and whether the notice applies to your course, centre, mode, and session.

### You cannot confirm the venue or mode

Use current official instructions and contact the relevant authorised channel early. Do not travel based only on an old screenshot or another student's arrangement.

### Payment completed but registration remains unavailable

Preserve the payment reference and confirmation, verify the payment status, and avoid repeating payment. Submit one clear report that connects the payment issue to the blocked exam-registration task.

### You lost the final registration record

Try to retrieve it through the current official workflow. Keep a fresh copy in more than one secure location because it may be useful when checking attendance, timetable, or result issues. If the main issue is getting the slip back out of the portal, go to [How to Print NOUN Course and Exam Registration Slips](/articles/noun-registration-slip-printout).

## Important Warnings

- Match the exam list with the courses on your saved registration slip.
- Check the exam mode, venue, and timetable for each registered course.
- Do not add an unfamiliar course simply because it appears on a screen.
- Save the completed exam-registration record before leaving the page.
- Recheck any later timetable or venue announcement that affects your courses.
