## Quick Answer: How to Pay NOUN School Fees

Start from the current official NOUN portal, fund your e-wallet through the authorised Remita workflow, confirm that the money reflects correctly, and then use the wallet for the specific semester charges that apply to your account. Do not generate a payment reference outside the official portal, and do not assume that funding the wallet alone means your semester registration is complete.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide explains how the NOUN e-wallet works, how to generate and pay a valid Remita reference, how to confirm that the payment reflected correctly, and how to respond safely when a payment or wallet balance looks wrong.

If you are specifically trying to decide whether a payment can be split, read [Can I Pay NOUN School Fees in Installments?](/articles/noun-installment-payment) before you assume there is an approved installment option.

## Requirements Before You Start

Prepare the records directly connected to this task before opening the portal or contacting support. Use your own current student record, confirm the relevant session or semester, and keep a secure folder for references and safe screenshots. Do not share passwords, one-time codes, payment-card details, or unrestricted portal access with anyone offering help.

## Understanding the NOUN E-Wallet System

At NOUN, you do not usually pay one final bill and become fully registered in a single click. You first fund the portal wallet, then use that balance for the compulsory semester fee, course registration, and exam registration that apply to your account. Think of the e-wallet as a controlled holding balance inside the official student portal.

This is why planning matters. Before generating a Remita Retrieval Reference (RRR), estimate the current amount you actually need and confirm the official charge on your own account. Multiple rushed payments can create confusion, duplicate charges, or an unclear support trail.

## Step-by-Step Guide: How to Pay NOUN School Fees

### Step 1: Log in through the official portal

Always begin from a current official NOUN page or a portal link you have independently verified. Open the student portal, choose the student login route, and enter your matric number and password.

### Step 2: Open the wallet-funding page

From the dashboard, locate the bursary or wallet area and choose the current funding option. Review the amount carefully before proceeding because this is the stage where students often carry over an old estimate or mistype a figure.

### Step 3: Generate your Remita reference inside the portal

Generate the RRR from the official portal, not from a random link, a cybercafe document, or a third-party site. Save the generated reference immediately. If the portal gives you a printable payment page, keep a copy before leaving the screen.

### Step 4: Complete the payment through Remita

You can usually pay online or at a bank branch. If you pay online, wait for the complete success flow and avoid closing the browser early. If you pay at a bank, keep the Remita receipt and any additional bank evidence that proves the payment was accepted.

### Step 5: Confirm that the wallet reflects correctly

Return to the official portal and review the wallet balance or payment-status area. Save the final reflected balance, the reference, and any status message that proves the payment succeeded before moving on to course or exam registration.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Generating an RRR outside the official student portal
- Funding the wallet without first confirming the current official charge
- Closing the payment flow before the success or redirect stage completes
- Paying again immediately because the wallet did not update within minutes
- Assuming wallet funding alone completes course or exam registration
- Handing your credentials or payment decisions to an unofficial helper

## Troubleshooting Payment Issues

### The bank debited you but the wallet did not update

Do not pay again immediately. Preserve the debit alert, transaction reference, receipt, date, amount, and the wallet view. Check the current official payment-status process, then submit one clear support report if the transaction remains unresolved.

### You generated a reference but did not complete payment

Confirm the current status of that reference before generating another one. An unpaid or expired reference is different from a successful payment that has not reflected. Use the official workflow to determine the next safe action.

### Your wallet balance is lower than expected

Compare the opening balance, funded amount, and every completed registration charge. A wallet balance can change after compulsory, course, or examination transactions. Treat your own current portal ledger as the working record.

### The amount in a blog differs from your portal

Use the blog only for planning. Fees and course selections can change, and another student may have a different programme or workload. Confirm the final payable amount through your own current official record.

### You are comparing undergraduate and postgraduate charges

Do not assume one fee article covers every student category. If your programme is PGD, Masters, or another postgraduate route, switch to [NOUN Postgraduate School Fees](/articles/noun-postgraduate-school-fees) before planning the next payment step.

### You need evidence for support

Create one folder containing the invoice or charge, payment reference, receipt, safe screenshot of the unresolved status, and a short timeline. This is more useful than sending repeated messages without references.
