## Quick Answer

**E-Exam (Electronic):**
- Taken on computer at exam centre
- Questions appear on screen
- You type answers on computer
- Automatic grading (mostly)
- Immediate feedback possible

**POP (Pen-on-Paper):**
- Traditional written exam
- Questions on printed paper
- You write answers by hand
- Manual grading by examiner
- Results take longer

**Which you take:** Depends on your course and program. Check your course registration for exam type.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide compares e-exams and POP exams, explains preparation differences, lists checks before exam registration, and shows what evidence to keep.


## E-Exam Format Explained

### E-Exam Advantages

- ✓ Faster grading (automatic)
- ✓ Results available sooner (sometimes)
- ✓ Cleaner, easier to read answers
- ✓ Can't lose paper
- ✓ Environment-friendly

### E-Exam Disadvantages

- ✗ Requires computer skills
- ✗ Technical issues possible (internet down, computer crash)
- ✗ Harder to show work for math/calculations
- ✗ Some feel rushed on computer
- ✗ Exam centre must have computers

### E-Exam Features

- Multiple choice questions
- Short answer questions (type in box)
- Essay questions (typing)
- Timer shows remaining time
- Can't go back to previous questions (usually)

## POP (Pen-on-Paper) Exam Format

### POP Advantages

- ✓ Familiar format (like school exams)
- ✓ Can write anywhere (more flexible)
- ✓ Can show all your work (good for math)
- ✓ Less technical stress
- ✓ Can draw diagrams, charts easily
- ✓ No computer required

### POP Disadvantages

- ✗ Longer to grade (manual)
- ✗ Results take 2-4 weeks (longer wait)
- ✗ Handwriting must be legible
- ✗ Paper can get lost
- ✗ Harder to make corrections
- ✗ Environmental impact (paper waste)

### POP Features

- Questions on printed booklet
- Answer on answer sheet or booklet
- May have multiple choice section
- May have essay section
- Examiner reads and grades manually

## Preparation Differences

### Prepare for E-Exam

- Practice typing (speed and accuracy matter)
- Take online practice exams
- Test your typing speed
- Practice using exam software (if available)
- Ensure you can navigate computer interface
- Mentally prepare for computer stress

### Prepare for POP

- Practice writing by hand (maintain legibility)
- Manage time with physical watch
- Practice essay writing
- Organize answers clearly on paper
- Bring extra pens (as backup)
- Practice working under time pressure

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can I choose which exam format?

Usually no. Format is assigned by course/program. Check during registration.

### Q2: What if computer crashes during e-exam?

Exam centre has backup plans:
1. IT staff helps restart
2. You continue with remaining time
3. Your answers saved automatically
4. In rare case, exam may be cancelled and rescheduled

### Q3: What if I fail both e-exam and POP in different courses?

You can retake the course (carryover). Same exam format usually applies.

### Q4: Is e-exam harder than paper exam?

Not inherently, but depends on your comfort with computers. Some find it easier, others harder.

### Q5: Can I request paper exam if course offers e-exam?

Rarely. Requests must be justified (accessibility needs, etc.). Ask your department.

### Q6: Which format is used more at NOUN?

Increasingly e-exams for efficiency, but many courses still use POP. Varies by program.

## Tips for Success

### For E-Exam

1. Arrive 30 minutes early
2. Test computer before exam starts
3. Read instructions carefully
4. Type clearly and accurately
5. Don't panic if computer feels slow
6. Use keyboard shortcuts if available

### For POP

1. Arrive 15 minutes early
2. Bring 2-3 pens + backup
3. Use blue or black pen only
4. Write clearly and neatly
5. Plan your essay time (allocation)
6. Leave space for corrections
