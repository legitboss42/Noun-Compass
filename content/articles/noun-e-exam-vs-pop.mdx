## The main difference between e-exam and POP

**E-Exam (Electronic):**
- Taken on computer at exam centre
- Questions appear on screen
- You type answers on computer
- Automatic grading (mostly)
- Immediate feedback possible

**POP (Pen-on-Paper):**
- Traditional written exam
- Questions on printed paper
- You write answers by hand
- Manual grading by examiner
- Results take longer

**Which you take:** Depends on your course and program. Check your course registration for exam type.

## What To Compare Before Exam Day

This guide compares e-exams and POP exams, explains preparation differences, lists checks before exam registration, and shows what evidence to keep.


## E-Exam Format Explained

### What changes in an e-exam

- Questions and responses are handled on a computer.
- Navigation, timing, review, and submission controls depend on the current exam interface.
- Students should practise reading and answering on-screen before the examination.

### E-Exam Features

- Multiple choice questions
- Short answer questions (type in box)
- Essay questions (typing)
- Timer shows remaining time
- Review controls depend on the instructions shown in the live examination

## POP (Pen-on-Paper) Exam Format

### What changes in a POP exam

- Questions and responses are handled on paper.
- Handwriting, page order, question numbering, and time management matter.
- Students should practise writing complete answers within the time allowed.

### POP Features

- Questions on printed booklet
- Answer on answer sheet or booklet
- May have multiple choice section
- May have essay section
- Examiner reads and grades manually

## Preparation Differences

### Prepare for E-Exam

- Practice typing (speed and accuracy matter)
- Take online practice exams
- Test your typing speed
- Practice using exam software (if available)
- Ensure you can navigate computer interface
- Mentally prepare for computer stress

### Prepare for POP

- Practice writing by hand (maintain legibility)
- Manage time with physical watch
- Practice essay writing
- Organize answers clearly on paper
- Bring extra pens (as backup)
- Practice working under time pressure

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can I choose which exam format?

Use the exam format attached to your course in the current registration or timetable. This page does not assume students can switch formats.

### Q2: What if computer crashes during e-exam?

Alert the invigilator immediately and follow the centre's recorded incident process. Do not restart, move seats, or assume your answers were saved without confirmation.

### Q3: What if I fail both e-exam and POP in different courses?

Check the result and current carryover guidance for each course. This article does not verify the format of a later attempt.

### Q4: Is e-exam harder than paper exam?

Not inherently, but depends on your comfort with computers. Some find it easier, others harder.

### Q5: Can I request paper exam if course offers e-exam?

Use the current official accessibility or exam-support route. This page does not verify which adjustments are available or who approves them.

### Q6: Which format is used more at NOUN?

The mix can change by course and examination period. Check the format assigned to your own registered courses.

## Tips for Success

### For E-Exam

1. Arrive 30 minutes early
2. Test computer before exam starts
3. Read instructions carefully
4. Type clearly and accurately
5. Don't panic if computer feels slow
6. Use keyboard shortcuts if available

### For POP

1. Arrive 15 minutes early
2. Bring 2-3 pens + backup
3. Use blue or black pen only
4. Write clearly and neatly
5. Plan your essay time (allocation)
6. Leave space for corrections

## Requirements Before You Start

Prepare from the exam format listed for each registered course. Keep the timetable or exam record and follow the centre instructions issued for that examination period.

## Step-by-Step Verification Process

1. Check the format assigned to each course on the current exam record.
2. Confirm the date, venue, and reporting instruction.
3. Practise with the assigned computer or handwritten response format.
4. Save the timetable or registration record you used.
5. Ask official exam support about a missing or conflicting format before exam day.

## Common Mistakes

- Preparing from another student's exam format.
- Ignoring the course code or session on the timetable.
- Travelling without saving the current venue and reporting instruction.
- Sharing an exam or portal login with a helper.
- Assuming a technical incident was recorded without telling the invigilator.

## Troubleshooting

If the exam format is missing or conflicts across records, save the course code, registration slip, timetable, and both labels. Request clarification before preparing for or travelling to the wrong format.
