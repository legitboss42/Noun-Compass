## When Should You Use Support Or e-Ticketing?

The verified results page itself told students what to do next when more detail was needed: `Click on Support (top right), eTicketing (Left) and login to see more results`. That makes Support and e-Ticketing part of the real result workflow. Use them after you have checked `My progress`, compared the deeper `Result statement` page, and prepared clear evidence.

## Why This Is Part Of The Results Workflow

Students sometimes treat support as the last resort after random trial and error. The live session showed something more specific. The `My progress` page itself directed students toward Support and e-Ticketing for more result detail.

That means the practical workflow is:

1. check the result surfaces first
2. then escalate through the route the system itself recommends

This is better than opening vague complaints before you know what the pages actually show.

## The Verified Support Route

The observed result-support path was:

1. inside the student portal, open `Support`
2. locate `Drop a complaint (e-Ticketing)`
3. move into the Support login page
4. log into the Support portal
5. use the support tools or `Result statement` action as needed

The verified Support login route was:

- `https://support.nou.edu.ng/login/`

The verified authenticated route was:

- `https://support.nou.edu.ng/user`

The session also confirmed that the Support dashboard shows ticket history, which means students can use it to review complaint activity after login.

## What To Check Before Opening A Result Complaint

Do these checks first:

- open `My progress`
- note the visible academic periods
- note whether the `Grade` column is present
- compare the deeper `Result statement` page if available
- record any registration-slip blocker if that page is relevant

That preparation helps you distinguish between:

- incomplete first-page results
- deeper ERP statement details
- registration-proof blockers

Without that preparation, support receives a broad complaint instead of a useful one.

## What Evidence To Prepare

Before you open support or e-ticketing for a result issue, collect:

- the exact page where the issue appears
- the course code if visible
- the academic period or batch if visible
- a safe screenshot of the affected area
- the wording of any blocker message
- the result of your comparison between `My progress` and the ERP statement page

For example, this is stronger:

`On My progress, the Grade column is visible for 2024_2 but the row shows a dash. I also checked Result statement through the Support portal and need help confirming whether the record is incomplete or pending.`

This is weaker:

`My result is not working.`

## When To Use Result Statement Before Filing A Complaint

In many cases, the Support portal helps even before you write a complaint because it contains `Result statement`. That action can resolve confusion by giving you a deeper record first.

So the best support habit is:

1. use Support as the bridge to more result detail
2. review the ERP statement page
3. only then decide whether a complaint is still necessary

This saves time and makes your complaint more precise if you still need one.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- using support before checking the main result pages
- writing a vague complaint with no course, period, or screenshot
- mixing registration-slip blockers and grade issues into one unclear story
- assuming support automatically proves transcript or correction workflows that were not verified
- escalating based on a friend's portal screenshot instead of your own account

## Troubleshooting

### I can see Support, but I do not know whether to complain yet

Use the Support route to reach `Result statement` first. That deeper page may answer your question before a complaint is necessary.

### I am not sure what exactly is wrong

Describe the page state, not your fear. For example, say whether the `Grade` column exists, whether rows show dashes, whether the ERP page opens, or whether the registration-slip route is blocked.

### I already know the issue is unresolved

Use the Support portal with a precise description and safe evidence. You can also review [NOUN Support Ticket Guide](/articles/noun-support-ticket-guide) for clearer escalation habits.
