## Quick Answer

In the verified session, the main NOUN result entry point was `My progress`, not a menu labeled `Results`. Open the logged-in student portal, choose `My progress`, and review the visible academic periods, result rows, and cumulative passed-credit summaries. If the page feels incomplete, follow the on-screen instruction to continue through `Support` and `eTicketing`.

## Why This Page Exists

One of the biggest result problems in the live session had nothing to do with grades themselves. It was navigation. Students looking for a menu called `Results` could easily miss the correct page because the actual label was `My progress`.

That small naming difference matters because it creates unnecessary search friction:

- `where is result on noun portal`
- `how to check noun result`
- `noun result portal`

This guide fixes that navigation gap using only the observed workflow.

## What Was Visible On My Progress

When `My progress` opened, the page loaded directly inside the logged-in portal. It showed:

- result/history rows
- a visible `Grade` column
- academic period headings such as `2024_1` and `2024_2`
- summary rows for `Total credit unit passed`
- `Cummulative credit unit passed`

That means `My progress` is not just a motivational dashboard label. It is the first real results surface.

At the same time, it had limits:

- no visible semester selector was shown
- visible grade cells on this account displayed dashes
- CGPA was not visibly confirmed on this page

So students should treat `My progress` as the first results checkpoint, not always the final answer.

## Step-By-Step: How To Find The Page

### Step 1: Log into the current student portal

Use the official NOUN student login and enter your own details. Make sure you are already inside the authenticated student area before looking for result history.

### Step 2: Look for My progress

In the observed portal, `My progress` was the top-menu label that opened the result/history area. Do not wait for the word `Results` to appear before you act.

### Step 3: Open the page and confirm history is loading

Once the page opens, check whether you can already see period labels and course rows. The live session showed periods inline rather than inside a dropdown.

### Step 4: Read the page for first-layer evidence

Use this page to confirm:

- whether academic periods are present
- whether result rows exist
- whether the `Grade` column is visible
- whether cumulative passed-credit totals are showing

This step matters because it tells you whether the portal is loading academic history at all.

### Step 5: Watch for the support instruction

The page included a direct note telling students to use `Support` and `eTicketing` to see more results. That is your clue that the first page is not the full workflow.

If you want the deeper record, continue with [How To Open Your NOUN Result Statement From The Support Portal](/articles/how-to-open-your-noun-result-statement-from-the-support-portal).

## What My Progress Can And Cannot Tell You

### What it can tell you

- you are in the right academic-history area
- academic periods are loading
- result columns exist
- cumulative passed-credit summaries are visible

### What it did not fully resolve in the observed session

- why visible grade cells showed dashes
- CGPA details
- current class of degree
- outstanding credit
- outstanding courses

Those deeper details were verified only after moving into the Support portal and opening the ERP result statement page.

## Why Students Get Confused Here

The confusion usually starts in one of three ways:

- the label `My progress` does not sound like the obvious result menu
- students expect a semester selector and do not see one
- the page shows a `Grade` column but not a clear final grade value

That combination makes it easy to think the wrong page opened when the real issue is simply that the workflow has a second stage.

## What To Do If The Page Looks Incomplete

Do not panic if `My progress` feels partial. The live page itself gave the next step:

1. use `Support`
2. open `Drop a complaint (eTicketing)` if needed
3. log into the Support portal
4. open `Result statement`

That deeper page is where the session verified fuller academic metrics.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- ignoring `My progress` because it does not say `Results`
- treating dashes in the `Grade` column as the final answer
- searching for a semester selector that was not visible on this account
- assuming the portal is broken before checking the Support route
- escalating without first noting the academic periods and page state visible on `My progress`
