## Where Are Outstanding Courses Listed?

In the verified NOUN workflow, outstanding courses were visible on the ERP statement-of-result page reached through the Support portal's `Result statement` action. They appeared in a dedicated `Outstanding Courses` section, not on the first `My progress` page.

## Why This Matters

Students often ask about carryover or unfinished courses, but the research boundary here is important. This phase did not verify an explicit carryover workflow or a dedicated carryover label. What it did verify is that the ERP result statement already contained an `Outstanding Courses` section.

That means this article stays factual:

- it shows where the section was found
- it explains how to use it
- it avoids unsupported claims about processes we did not observe

## The Verified Route

Use this path:

1. log into the NOUN student portal
2. open `My progress`
3. use the Support route the page recommends
4. log into `support.nou.edu.ng`
5. click `Result statement`
6. scroll through the ERP page until you reach `Outstanding Courses`

This route matters because the first result screen did not expose the same level of unfinished-requirement detail.

## What The Outstanding Courses Section Looked Like

The live session confirmed a dedicated `Outstanding Courses` area on the ERP result statement page. The section presented course information in a structured list rather than a vague summary note.

## How To Use The Section Safely

### Step 1: Reach the ERP result statement page

Do not look for this section on the first portal screen. It was verified only on the deeper statement page.

### Step 2: Scroll until you find the section

The ERP page contained multiple academic blocks, including grades, summary statistics, performance standing, and outstanding courses. Move through the page carefully rather than assuming the first visible panel is the only important one.

### Step 3: Read the course entries literally

Record what the system itself shows. Do not add labels like `carryover` unless the page itself uses them.

### Step 4: Compare with your registration and planning workflow

Once the outstanding-course list is visible, compare it with your current academic planning:

## What This Section Does And Does Not Confirm

### It does confirm

- the system can display an `Outstanding Courses` section
- that section lives on the ERP result-statement page
- students can use it as part of academic review

### It does not confirm

- a full carryover registration workflow
- how every outstanding course should be treated in future semesters
- transcript or result-correction processes

Those remain separate research tasks.
