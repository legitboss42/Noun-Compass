## Where Should You Check NOUN Results?

The verified NOUN results workflow did not stop at one page. In the observed session, students started inside the main student portal, opened `My progress`, reviewed the visible history rows, then followed the on-page instruction to use `Support` and `eTicketing` for more result detail. After logging into the Support portal, the strongest academic record surface was `Result statement`, which opened the ERP page at `erp.nou.edu.ng/stsor`.

Use this order:

1. Log into the student portal.
2. Open `My progress`.
3. Review the visible result/history rows.
4. Follow the note that points you to `Support` and `eTicketing`.
5. Log into the Support portal.
6. Open `Result statement`.
7. Review the ERP statement page for grades, CGPA, class of degree, outstanding credit, and outstanding courses.

## Step 1: Start In The Student Portal And Open My Progress

The first important lesson from the research session is simple: the results entry point was not called `Results`. The visible label was `My progress`.

That matters because many students naturally search for a clear result menu and miss the correct entry point while still being inside the right portal.

When `My progress` opened, the page loaded academic history directly inside the student portal. It did not look like a separate public checker. It showed:

- academic period labels such as `2024_1` and `2024_2`
- rows for course records
- a visible `Grade` column
- cumulative credit summaries

The page also showed an important limitation: the visible grade cells on this account were dashes. So the portal proved that a grade field exists, but it did not fully resolve the grade problem on its own.

That is why this guide does not stop at `My progress`.

## Step 2: Read My Progress For The First Layer Of Evidence

`My progress` is still useful even when it does not show everything. It gave us three valuable checks:

1. historical academic periods were visible without a semester selector
2. result rows were already tied to the student's academic history
3. cumulative passed-credit summaries were visible on the page

This means `My progress` is the correct first screen to answer questions like:

- am I in the right part of the portal?
- is my academic history loading at all?
- are the expected periods visible?
- is the grade column present even if the values are incomplete?

Students often skip this step because they want a final answer immediately. That creates confusion later. The safer sequence is to collect the first layer of evidence from `My progress`, then move to the deeper result surface.

If you only need help with this first step, continue with [How To Find NOUN Results On My Progress](/articles/how-to-find-noun-results-on-my-progress).

## Step 3: Follow The Built-In Support Instruction

One of the strongest findings from the live session was that the result workflow itself pointed students toward another route. The note on the `My progress` page said:

`Click on Support (top right), eTicketing (Left) and login to see more results`

That sentence makes Support part of the documented result route when the first page does not provide enough detail.

So the verified student path became:

1. open `My progress`
2. review the visible record
3. use `Support`
4. locate `Drop a complaint (e-Ticketing)`
5. move into the separate Support login

This is one reason students can feel the results process is confusing. The strongest academic record view is not on the same surface as the first result page.

For the exact bridge into that next stage, read [How To Open Your NOUN Result Statement From The Support Portal](/articles/how-to-open-your-noun-result-statement-from-the-support-portal).

## Step 4: Log Into The Support Portal And Open Result Statement

The observed Support login route was:

- `https://support.nou.edu.ng/login/`

That action opened a student-specific result page in a new tab. The confirmed destination was:

This matters because it is the strongest verified academic-record surface in the whole workflow. It is the page that moved the session from a partial history screen to a fuller statement of result.

## What To Do When Grades Still Do Not Show Clearly

The observed `My progress` screen showed the `Grade` column, but the visible rows displayed dashes. That creates one of the most important student search problems in this cluster: the grade area exists, but the value does not look resolved.

The safest sequence is:

1. confirm that `My progress` is loading the expected academic periods
2. move into the Support portal
3. open `Result statement`
4. compare the deeper ERP table with the earlier portal screen
5. save safe evidence before escalating

Do not jump straight to assumptions like:

- the result is permanently missing
- the portal is broken for everyone
- you need to register the course again immediately

Instead, compare the two surfaces first.

If this is your exact issue, continue with [Why Your NOUN Result Grade Is Not Showing](/articles/why-your-noun-result-grade-is-not-showing).

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- searching the portal for a `Results` label and ignoring `My progress`
- stopping at the first result screen and never opening the Support route
- assuming dashes in the `Grade` column explain the whole issue
- expecting CGPA on the first page when it was only verified on the ERP statement page
- using unverified carryover or transcript advice from random sources
- escalating without screenshots, course codes, or the exact page where the problem appears

## Troubleshooting

### I can find My progress but not the full result record

That is exactly what the observed workflow prepared for. Use the on-page note, open `Support`, and continue to `Result statement`.

### My grade column is there, but I only see dashes

Do not stop there. Compare the first portal screen with the ERP statement page before deciding the record is missing.

### I need CGPA and class of degree

Check the Support portal route and open `Result statement`. Those metrics were verified there, not on the first result page.

### I need to prove my registration history, but the slip page is blocked

Treat that as a separate blocker tied to current-semester registration. Review the registration workflow before assuming the result record itself is the only problem.

### I still need help after checking both pages

Use the official support path and prepare a clear message.
