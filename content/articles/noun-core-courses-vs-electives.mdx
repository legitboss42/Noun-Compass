## How to tell core courses from electives

**Core Courses (Required):**
- Mandatory for your degree
- All students in program must take
- Cannot skip or substitute
- Count toward your degree requirements
- Usually 60–70% of total courses

**Elective Courses (Optional):**
- You choose which to take
- Usually 2–4 per program
- Must meet prerequisite (if any)
- Allow specialization interest
- Usually 20–30% of total courses

---


## What To Compare On Your Course List

This guide explains how to identify core courses, how to treat electives, why credit units matter, and what to verify before submitting course registration.


## Understanding Core Courses

### What Core Courses Cover

Core courses are the foundation of your program:
- Core theory and concepts
- Essential practical skills
- Skills employers expect
- Material for your field

**Example:** If studying Business Administration, core courses might include:
- Financial Accounting
- Business Management
- Economics
- Organizational Behavior

### Requirements

- ✓ You MUST take them
- ✓ You must meet the programme's pass requirement
- ✓ You MUST register for them
- ✓ Cannot skip to fewer courses

---

## Understanding Elective Courses

### What Electives Allow

Electives let you customize your degree:
- Choose courses matching your interests
- Specialize in area you care about
- Develop unique skill set
- Pursue double specialization (sometimes)

**Example:** In a Business Administration program with Finance electives:
- Investment Analysis
- Banking & Finance
- Financial Planning

### Your Flexibility

- ✓ You CHOOSE which electives
- ✓ You have options (usually pick from list)
- ✓ Can choose different electives each semester
- ✓ Can change mind about specialty

---

## Strategic Elective Selection

### Questions to Ask

1. **What interests me?** Choose courses aligned with interests
2. **What career path?** Choose electives that build toward career goals
3. **What skills do I need?** Choose practical electives
4. **What are prerequisites?** Make sure I meet them
5. **Is this course offered?** Check semester schedule

### Example Strategy

If you want Finance career:
- Choose Finance, Investment, Banking electives
- Skip HR or Marketing electives
- Build marketable specialization

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Can I take more than required electives?

Usually no, you're limited to registered credit units. But you can try requesting overload.

### Q2: What if I don't like my chosen elective?

During add/drop period, you can usually change. Check deadline.

### Q3: Do electives have lower standards than core courses?

No, grading is equally rigorous. Elective grades count same as core courses.

### Q4: Can I substitute core with elective?

Usually no. Core courses are fixed requirements.

## Requirements Before You Start

Keep the current curriculum, result statement, level, semester, and proposed course list beside you. Do not give another person unrestricted access to register on your behalf.

## Step-by-Step Verification Process

1. Open the current curriculum for your programme and level.
2. Mark each proposed course as core, elective, carryover, or unclear.
3. Check the semester and units for every code.
4. Compare the final portal selection with that list.
5. Save the submitted slip and report any unexplained mismatch.

## Common Mistakes

- Copying another student's elective choices.
- Registering a course before checking its code, semester, and curriculum status.
- Submitting without keeping the selected course list.
- Letting someone else choose courses through your account.
- Resubmitting because the confirmation page was not read carefully.

## Troubleshooting

If the portal classification conflicts with the curriculum, save the course code, programme, level, semester, and selected list. Ask which classification applies before submitting or paying for the course.

## Official Sources and Verification Notes

The curriculum and your current student record decide whether a course is core, elective, or unresolved. Use this page to compare them, not to replace them.

## Practical Student Workflow

Before registering, list the courses shown for your programme and mark the ones that are compulsory. Then compare electives with your current programme instruction instead of choosing every available elective. If two students in different programmes choose different electives, both may be correct. The safe question is not ?what did my friend register?? but ?what does my current programme record require??

Mark each course on your proposed list as core, elective, carryover, or unclear. Match the code and units to the current curriculum before submitting; a course that appears on-screen is not automatically required for your programme.
