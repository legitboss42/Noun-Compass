## What to check first

If your NOUN e-wallet balance is missing, do not pay again until you have checked the transaction status, the RRR or payment reference, your bank record, and the wallet history shown in your own portal.

**First step:** Save the screen you can see now, refresh the portal once, and compare it with your payment receipt. If the records still disagree, prepare the reference, amount, date, and safe screenshots for official support.

## Step-by-Step Guide: How to Fix Your Missing E-Wallet Balance

### Step 1: Clear Your Browser Cache (Takes 2 minutes)

Your browser might be displaying cached information from before your payment.

**For Chrome:**
1. Press `Ctrl + Shift + Delete` to open Clear Browsing Data
2. Select "All time" under Time range
3. Check boxes for Cookies and Cached images and files
4. Click "Clear data"
5. Log out of NOUN portal and log back in

**For Firefox:**
1. Press `Ctrl + Shift + Delete` to open Clear Recent History
2. Select "Everything" under Time range
3. Make sure "Cookies" and "Cache" are checked
4. Click "Clear Now"
5. Log out of NOUN portal and log back in

**For Safari:**
1. Click Safari → Preferences → Privacy
2. Click "Manage Website Data"
3. Search for "nouonline"
4. Select it and click "Remove"
5. Log out and log back in

**Important:** After clearing cache, wait 5-10 minutes before checking your balance again.

### Step 2: Check Payment Status on NOUN Portal

1. Log in to the NOUN portal (the official NOUN student portal)
2. Click "My Profile" in the top-right corner
3. Select "Payments" or "Wallet Management"
4. Look for "Check Payment Status" or "Transaction History"
5. Search for your payment using the date range

**What to look for:**
- Transaction ID, RRR, or other payment reference shown on your receipt
- Status: "Successful," "Pending," or "Failed"
- Amount and timestamp
- Payment channel shown on the record

If the status shows **"Pending"**: Do not create another payment. Keep the reference and use the current guidance shown by the payment provider or official support route.

If the status shows **"Failed"**: Compare the portal status with your bank record before deciding what happened. [Proceed to Step 4](#step-4-verify-rrr-and-payment-reference).

If **no transaction appears**: Check the bank record and the reference you used. A missing portal entry does not by itself prove whether the bank completed, reversed, or never submitted the payment.

### Step 3: Verify Wallet Balance Using the Manage Wallet Page

1. Log into your NOUN portal
2. Navigate to "Manage Wallet" (typically under Student Dashboard or Payments)
3. Your current balance should display prominently
4. Check the "Wallet History" tab to see all recent transactions
5. Look for your most recent payment in the list

**If your payment appears in history but not in balance:**
This suggests the funds were applied to a specific course or fee. [Check your course registration fees](#common-mistakes).

**If your payment doesn't appear anywhere:**
Your payment may not have been credited yet. Wait until the next business day, then [contact support](#important-warnings).

### Step 4: Verify RRR and Payment Reference

**To find your RRR:**
1. Check your bank statement or SMS confirmation
2. Look for text like: "RRR: 123456789012"
3. Copy this number exactly (do not alter it)

**To verify the RRR on NOUN portal:**
1. Go to "Verify RRR" or "Payment Verification" page
2. Enter your RRR
3. Enter the exact amount you paid
4. Click "Verify"

The portal should confirm whether:
- ✓ RRR is valid and funds are allocated
- ✓ RRR matches the amount you entered
- ✗ RRR is invalid or doesn't exist
- ✗ RRR amount doesn't match

If verification fails, contact [NOUN IT support](https://www.noun.edu.ng/contact) immediately with your bank reference.

### Step 5: Contact Support with Documentation

If your balance is still missing after following steps 1-4, you'll need to contact NOUN support officially.

**What to prepare:**
- Your Transaction ID or RRR
- Screenshot of your bank confirmation
- Screenshot of the "Check Payment Status" page showing "Failed" or "Pending"
- Exact amount paid
- Date of payment
- Your student ID

**Contact channels:**
1. **NOUN IT Portal Support:** Log in → Help Center → Submit Ticket
2. **Email:** [Check official NOUN contact page](https://www.noun.edu.ng/contact)
3. **Phone:** Call NOUN main line (verify current number on official website)
4. **Student Centre:** Visit your nearest NOUN study centre in person

## Mistakes That Make A Payment Harder To Trace

### Treating a delay as proof of failure

A balance that has not changed does not tell you whether the payment is pending, failed, reversed, or attached to another charge. Read the actual status and compare it with your receipt before taking another action.

### Repeating the same browser session

Refresh once or sign in again through the official portal. A second device or browser can help rule out a display problem, but it cannot confirm the payment outcome on its own.

### Creating another payment before checking the first

A second attempt can create a duplicate charge or a second reference. Confirm the first transaction through the portal, provider, or official support before retrying.

### Losing the transaction reference

The RRR or transaction reference helps the bank, payment provider, or official support team trace the payment. Save it with the amount, date, receipt, and portal status.

### Guessing where the money went

Use the wallet or transaction history available on your account and compare each entry with the receipt. If the records do not explain the difference, report the mismatch instead of assigning a cause yourself.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How long does a NOUN e-wallet balance take to update?

**A:** This page does not verify one processing time for every bank, payment channel, or semester. Use the status attached to your transaction and the current guidance from the payment provider or official NOUN support.

### Q2: Why does my balance show zero even though I just paid?

**A:** Likely reasons:
1. Payment is still processing (most common)
2. Browser showing cached old balance—clear cache
3. Funds were immediately allocated to course fees
4. Payment failed silently

Always check "Check Payment Status" to see the actual transaction status.

### Q3: I paid one amount but my wallet shows a lower balance. Where did the difference go?

**A:** Common explanations:
- Some funds were already allocated to course materials
- A portion went to compulsory fees automatically
- The difference might appear under "Pending" or "Allocated" sections

Check your "Wallet History" tab to see the breakdown.

### Q4: My bank says they charged me twice, but NOUN only shows one payment. What do I do?

**A:** Contact NOUN support immediately with:
- Both bank transaction IDs
- Screenshots of both bank charges
- Your NOUN portal transaction details

Do not assume a refund timetable from this article. Preserve the evidence, confirm the current portal status, and use the official support route before repeating any payment.

### Q5: Can NOUN refund my payment if I paid by mistake?

**A:** A refund may be possible, but this page does not verify a universal timeline or outcome. Submit the current official request route with your Transaction ID or RRR, save the acknowledgement, and avoid assuming how quickly the money will return.

### Q6: Is my e-wallet money safe? Can it be stolen?

**A:** Yes, your wallet is safe. NOUN uses:
- Bank-grade encryption
- Secure payment gateways (Remita, Flutterwave)
- Transaction verification systems

Your wallet funds are only accessible:
- By you (with your login credentials)
- When applied to official NOUN courses and fees
- During refund requests to your bank account

Never share your portal password or OTP codes.

### Q7: What if NOUN's system shows my balance but my bank didn't charge me?

**A:** This shouldn't happen with official payments. If it does:
1. Check your bank again (sometimes charges appear with a delay)
2. Check if you might have paid from a virtual card or different account
3. Contact NOUN support to verify they actually received the payment
4. If confirmed as a system error, NOUN will likely reverse it

### Q8: Can I check my e-wallet balance without logging into the portal?

**A:** No. Your balance is personal account information only accessible:
- By logging into the official NOUN student portal
- Through an official NOUN mobile app (if available)
- By visiting your study centre in person

Be cautious of third-party websites claiming to check balances.
