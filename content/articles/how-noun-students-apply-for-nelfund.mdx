## Quick Answer

The verified public NELFUND path for NOUN students currently starts on the official NELFUND portal, then moves through educational verification and JAMB-based verification before continuing to email verification. The parts we can state confidently from the Phase 7.4 research are:

1. open the official NELFUND portal
2. start registration
3. confirm nationality
4. verify educational information
5. select institution
6. enter matric number
7. complete JAMB-based verification with JAMB registration number and date of birth
8. continue to email verification

What we should not do is invent unverified later steps or promise what happens in every case after that point.

## What The Verified Public Flow Looks Like

The Phase 7.4 workflow map confirmed that the public registration path currently includes these stages.

### Step 1: Confirm you are on the student-loan application flow

The public portal identifies itself as the student-loan application platform. This is the first trust check. Before you type anything sensitive, confirm that you are on the real NELFUND surface.

### Step 2: Begin registration

The visible call to action moves students into the registration flow. The research confirmed that the sequence is structured, not random.

### Step 3: Confirm nationality

The current public flow begins with a nationality confirmation step. That does not resolve NOUN-specific eligibility by itself, but it is part of the visible verified path.

### Step 4: Verify educational information

This is one of the most important stages for NOUN students because it is where the application begins tying your identity to your school record.

The verified public flow shows:

- `Select Institution`
- `Matric Number`

That means students should expect the application to check school-linked data early, not as a vague later add-on.

### Step 5: Watch for missing-information or mismatch states

The public flow also showed a visible `missing information` state. This is important because it proves the system can stop or warn a student before the application continues cleanly.

That does not mean every mismatch has the same cause. It does mean students should not assume that reaching the form is the same as passing verification.

### Step 6: Complete JAMB-based verification

The public flow then verified a JAMB step that asks for:

- JAMB registration number
- date of birth

The next visible action is `Verify JAMB Profile`.

This is why the JAMB issue keeps coming up in NELFUND discussions around NOUN. It is not a rumor. The JAMB-based verification step is part of the current public flow.

For a focused explanation of that part, read [Why NELFUND Requests A JAMB Registration Number](/articles/why-nelfund-requests-a-jamb-registration-number).

### Step 7: Continue to email verification

After successful JAMB verification, the public flow showed a continuation step into email verification.

This is the edge of what the Phase 7.4 research safely confirmed in detail. The article should stop at that boundary rather than fabricating what happens after email verification for every NOUN student.

## Common Mistakes During Application

### Mistake 1: Treating the public flow like a casual form

The presence of institution verification and JAMB verification means the system is checking linked records, not just collecting interest.

### Mistake 2: Starting the application without checking your own records first

If your school and identity details are inconsistent, you are more likely to misread a mismatch as a random portal failure.

### Mistake 3: Turning a friend's success into your own certainty

The official NOUN post confirmed that 90 students reached approved disbursement. That proves participation. It does not prove that every student record is ready in the same way.

### Mistake 4: Oversharing financial or account access

Do not give full account control, passwords, one-time codes, or unrestricted personal data to someone who says they can `help you apply`.

### Mistake 5: Believing that unverified NOUN-specific rules are already settled

The Phase 7.4 research deliberately left several NOUN-specific questions as partial or unverified. The application guide should respect that boundary.

## Troubleshooting The First Application Steps

### The portal opens but you do not know if it is official

Start again from [portal.nelf.gov.ng](https://portal.nelf.gov.ng/) and avoid forwarded or shortened links.

### You do not know what record the matric number should match

The safest answer from the research is that the public flow expects institution-linked educational verification. Use your current NOUN student record and avoid copying another student's assumptions.

### The form asks for JAMB details and you were not expecting that

That is a verified part of the current public flow. It is not proof of every regularization claim, but it is a real step in the application sequence.

### You hit a missing-information warning

Do not rush into a second attempt without understanding the first problem. The public flow itself shows that missing-information states can happen. Save what you can, note the exact stage, and compare your NOUN record carefully before escalating.
