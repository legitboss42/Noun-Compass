## Quick Answer

NOUN graduation and convocation can involve clearance, document-processing, certificate, transcript, or ceremony-related charges, but the exact current amount must be confirmed through official NOUN channels before payment.

Do not rely on an old fee list. Start by confirming that you are academically eligible, then check the current official graduation or convocation instruction for your session.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide explains:

- how to verify whether you are at the right stage for graduation-related payments
- how to read graduation and convocation cost questions safely
- what records to keep before paying
- how to escalate a mismatch without guessing

## Step-By-Step Guide: Verifying Graduation And Convocation Costs

### Step 1: Confirm academic readiness first

Before paying any graduation-related charge, verify your academic standing. Confirm that you have completed the required courses, resolved outstanding results, and cleared any obvious registration or support problem that could block final processing.

Use:

- [How To Check NOUN Results](/articles/how-to-check-noun-results)

- [How To Check Outstanding Courses On NOUN Result Statement](/articles/how-to-check-outstanding-courses-on-noun-result-statement)

### Step 2: Read the current official instruction

Graduation instructions are highly update-sensitive. Look for the current NOUN graduation or convocation notice and confirm the session, deadline, payment workflow, and required documents.

### Step 3: Identify the visible charge lines

Depending on the current instruction and your programme, graduation-related items may include clearance, convocation registration, transcript request, certificate processing, study-centre documentation, or other official charge lines.

The names and amounts can change. Your job is not to memorize old figures. It is to confirm the current charge attached to your own record.

### Step 4: Pay only through official channels

Use only the payment workflow specified by NOUN. Save the RRR or payment reference, bank confirmation, and portal confirmation. If a charge is study-centre related, confirm the approved process with the centre before payment.

### Step 5: Confirm your name and document details

After payment and clearance, confirm that your name, programme, matric number, and graduation details are correct wherever NOUN provides a verification list or portal record.

### Step 6: Keep receipts until collection is complete

Keep every receipt and confirmation until your certificate, transcript, or other graduation document has been collected or formally confirmed.

## Common Mistakes

- paying because another student said a charge was compulsory without checking the current instruction
- confusing academic eligibility with ceremony readiness
- using an old convocation list as if it were the current session notice
- paying before verifying your final result and outstanding-course status
- losing payment references before the graduation process is fully complete

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are all graduation-related charges mandatory?

Not safely enough to say as a universal rule. Confirm the current official notice and the charge lines attached to your own record before treating any item as compulsory.

### Can I pay everything at once?

This page does not verify a universal recommended order or staged timeline. Follow the current official workflow instead of relying on an old payment plan.

### Should I pay if my result record still has a problem?

Pause and verify the academic record first. A result, outstanding-course, or clearance mismatch can affect the value of paying too early.

### Do old convocation costs still apply this year?

Treat them only as historical context, not as current pricing. Session-specific official instructions should control the final decision.

## Important Warnings

- Do not pay graduation-related charges from an unofficial list or forwarded message.
- Do not assume that another student's final-year workflow matches yours.
- Do not ignore unresolved results or outstanding-course issues before paying.
- Keep payment evidence until the full process is complete.
