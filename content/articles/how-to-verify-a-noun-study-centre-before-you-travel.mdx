## What Should You Confirm Before Travelling?

Before travelling to any NOUN study centre, do **not** rely on one directory row, one map pin, or one forwarded message. Use a verification sequence:

1. check whether the centre was fully verified or only partially verified
2. confirm the exact centre name through official NOUN signals
3. match the centre visit to your real student task
4. confirm the current detail again before travelling
5. keep a record of the visit reason and next instruction

That process is safer than travelling because a centre name "looks official."

## Why This Guide Matters

Study-centre content is one of the easiest places for student sites to become inaccurate. Addresses get copied. Old blog posts stay indexed. Third-party lists blend real centres with weakly supported ones. Students then lose time and money travelling to the wrong place.

The 19 June 2026 centre review showed why this extra check is necessary:

- some locations were strongly verified
- some were only partial
- some popular city searches still did not justify publication as confirmed centre pages

This guide helps you move from finding a centre name to confirming the address, contact, service, and travel details that matter for your visit.

The goal is simple: begin with the most carefully filtered list, not the biggest list.

### Step 2: Check the verification status

Ask one question immediately: was the centre marked:

- `VERIFIED`
- `PARTIALLY VERIFIED`
- `UNVERIFIED`

That label changes how much confidence you should place in the row.

#### If a centre is verified

It cleared a stronger official-evidence threshold and is safer to use as your starting point.

#### If a centre is partially verified

Treat it as a lead, not a final instruction. Partial does not mean fake, but it does mean "check again before you move."

#### If a centre is unverified

Do not travel because of that row alone.

### Step 3: Match the centre to your actual task

This is where many students make mistakes. They search for a centre when what they really need is a workflow.

Ask what your real task is:

- [admission](/articles/how-to-apply-for-noun-admission)
- [course registration](/articles/how-to-register-noun-courses)
- [TMA/eLearn](/articles/noun-elearn-and-tma-guide)
- [exam registration](/articles/noun-exam-registration-guide)
- [results](/articles/how-to-check-noun-results)
- [support](/articles/noun-support-ticket-guide)

If the task has a clear online or support workflow first, use that before assuming the physical centre is your starting point.

### Step 4: Confirm the current centre detail through official signals

Even a verified centre should be checked again before the trip. A centre can be real while the copied direction, contact route, or local service arrangement is outdated.

You are checking for:

- exact centre name
- current location detail
- whether the centre still appears in official NOUN signals
- whether your specific task still belongs there

### Step 5: Travel with only the records you need

Do not carry unnecessary personal material because you are "just in case" preparing for every possible issue.

Take only what matches the task, such as:

- admission reference
- registration slip or receipt
- result complaint reference
- support ticket detail

If your issue is an academic record problem, prepare with [How To Open Your NOUN Result Statement From The Support Portal](/articles/how-to-open-your-noun-result-statement-from-the-support-portal) or [Why Your NOUN Result Grade Is Not Showing](/articles/why-your-noun-result-grade-is-not-showing) first.

### Step 6: Save the next instruction after the visit

A useful centre visit should end with something concrete:

- a redirected workflow
- a support route
- a portal step
- a clearer office instruction

Write it down. Do not depend on memory after the trip.

## A Simple Decision Tree For Students

If you want a fast way to decide whether a trip is sensible, use this logic:

### The centre is verified and the task clearly needs local follow-up

Travel may make sense after you confirm the current detail one more time.

### The centre is partially verified but the issue is urgent

Pause and see whether a stronger verified centre, [support ticket](/articles/noun-support-ticket-guide), or [results workflow](/articles/how-to-check-noun-results) can narrow the problem first.

### The centre is unverified

Do not plan a real trip around that row alone.

### The issue is really portal-based

Use the workflow guide first. For example:

- [course registration](/articles/how-to-register-noun-courses)

That sequence can save you a wasted trip.

## What A Good Verification Record Looks Like

After you check a centre, keep a short record with:

- centre name
- verification status
- date checked
- task you are trying to complete
- current source or instruction you relied on

This is especially useful if the trip later turns into a support or follow-up issue. A clean record is often more valuable than a vague memory that "the centre was mentioned somewhere."

## Signs A Centre Claim Needs More Caution

Be extra careful when:

- the row exists only on one third-party directory
- the name sounds official but the page does not show direct NOUN support
- a special or correctional centre is mentioned without strong official confirmation
- a location is popular in search results but weak in the verification report
- you are being rushed to travel or pay without a clear official path

## Mistakes Students Make Before Travelling

- trusting a search snippet instead of a full verification note
- confusing `partial` with `fully confirmed`
- assuming every centre handles every issue
- taking a centre trip for a task that already has a better portal or support workflow
- mixing up general centres with special or correctional centres

## What To Do When The Evidence Is Mixed

Sometimes the right answer is not "yes, travel" or "no, ignore it." Sometimes it is:

- keep the centre on your shortlist
- do one more check
- use the stronger verified centre first
- shift to support or portal guidance until the centre need is clearer

That is exactly how students should handle partially verified rows like `Wuse II Study Centre Abuja`, `Mushin Study Centre Lagos`, or `Ilaro Community Study Centre Ogun`.

## Special Warning About Special And Correctional Centres

Do not treat special-centre or correctional-centre rows casually. Those rows often need a different level of caution and may not serve the general student audience in the same way as a standard centre. Read [Special And Correctional NOUN Study Centres Guide](/articles/special-and-correctional-noun-study-centres-guide) before relying on one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the first thing I should check before travelling to a NOUN centre?

Check whether the centre was marked verified, partially verified, or unverified in the 19 June 2026 review.

### Is a partially verified centre fake?

Not necessarily. It means the evidence was not strong enough to treat it like a fully confirmed centre detail.

### Should I use a centre visit for every NOUN problem?

No. Many issues are better handled first through the portal, eLearn, result workflow, or support route.

### What if the centre is verified but the task still feels unclear?

Pause and match the trip to your actual student workflow first. A verified centre name does not solve the wrong problem.

### Which page should I read after this one?

Read the [national verified list](/articles/full-list-of-verified-noun-study-centres-in-nigeria), then the location page or workflow guide that matches your task.
