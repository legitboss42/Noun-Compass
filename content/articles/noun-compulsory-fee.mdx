## Check the charge on your current bill

The NOUN portal may show a charge labelled `Compulsory Fee` in your current fee or registration breakdown. Confirm the label, amount, session, and payment status on your own account before paying. This article does not treat an old fee list as proof of what you owe now.

## What To Check On Your Fee Breakdown

This page explains how to identify the charge, separate it from course or examination fees, and preserve the right payment evidence. It does not assign the money to specific services unless a current official schedule does so.

## Why Students Search For This

Students usually arrive here after seeing an unfamiliar line on a bill. The useful question is not what another student paid, but what the current line on your own record says.

---

## How To Read The Compulsory-Fee Line

The charge may appear beside course registration, examination registration, or programme-specific items. Read each row separately. A label on the portal shows what NOUN is charging; it does not, by itself, provide a detailed breakdown of how the money is used.

If you need that explanation, look for a current official fee schedule or announcement that names the components. Do not fill the gap with a list copied from another school or an older semester.

Do not use a fixed amount from an old article, screenshot, social media post, or another student's portal as your own payable amount. Your current portal breakdown is the figure that matters.

---

## Questions The Portal Line Does Not Answer

The label alone does not establish whether the amount is uniform across programmes, what services it funds, whether it is refundable, or what happens after withdrawal. Those answers require a current official fee or refund policy.

---

## Common Misunderstandings

**"Can I skip compulsory fee?"**
Do not omit or alter a current charge without official confirmation. If the line looks wrong, save the bill and ask NOUN to clarify it before payment.

**"What if I don't use library or IT services?"**
Use a current official breakdown if one is available. This page does not infer the purpose from the label.

**"Is this on top of tuition?"**
Check whether your portal lists the charges as separate rows and compare the total with the generated bill.

**"Why is compulsory fee same for all programs?"**
Do not assume the amount is uniform from another student's record. Compare only with the current official schedule for your programme and session.

---

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: When is compulsory fee charged?

Check the session and semester attached to the charge on your own portal.

### Q2: Can compulsory fee be refunded if I withdraw?

This page does not verify a universal refund window. Follow the current official refund or withdrawal policy before expecting money to be returned.

### Q3: Is compulsory fee different for undergraduate vs postgraduate?

The amount may differ by programme or charge type. Use the current official breakdown rather than a general comparison.

### Q4: Can I dispute the compulsory fee amount?

If the amount differs from the current official schedule or appears under the wrong session, document the mismatch and request clarification before paying.

## Important Warnings

⚠️ **Do NOT:**
- Assume you can skip compulsory fee to save money (you can't—it's mandatory)
- Pay only course tuition without compulsory fee (payment will be rejected)
- Confuse compulsory fee with elective course fees (they're separate)
- Expect refund of compulsory fee after deadline (non-refundable)

✓ **DO:**
- Factor compulsory fee into your semester budget
- Pay it on time during registration window
- Keep proof of payment
- Verify exact amount through official portal

## Use The Charge On Your Own Bill

The compulsory fee is part of the semester cost structure, but the amount should never be guessed. Before paying, open your own current registration or fee breakdown, confirm the session and semester, and save the final receipt after payment.

If the compulsory-fee line looks unusual, compare it with the official fee information available to you and ask NOUN support or your study centre before repeating payment.


## Requirements Before You Start

Open the fee breakdown for your own session and save the charge label, amount, and bill before paying. Keep account and payment credentials private.

## Step-by-Step Verification Process

1. Open the fee breakdown for your own account and session.
2. Find the exact `Compulsory Fee` label and amount.
3. Compare it with the current official fee schedule if one is available.
4. Save the bill and payment receipt.
5. Report a mismatched label, amount, or session before paying again.

## Common Mistakes

- Copying the amount from another student's bill.
- Paying a charge attached to the wrong session.
- Leaving without saving the fee breakdown and receipt.
- Giving a helper access to the wallet or payment account.
- Generating a second reference before tracing the first payment.

## Troubleshooting

If the compulsory-fee line is missing or differs from the current schedule, save the bill, session, label, and amount. Ask official finance support to explain that specific difference before generating another payment reference.

## Official Sources and Verification Notes

This page explains how to read the charge; it does not set the fee or refund policy. Use the current NOUN bill and official policy for the final amount and terms.
