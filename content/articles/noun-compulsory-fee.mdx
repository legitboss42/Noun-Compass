## Quick Answer

NOUN compulsory fee is a mandatory charge applied every semester that covers essential university services and operations. The current amount must be confirmed inside your own official portal before payment.

**What it covers:**
- Academic administration
- Library services
- IT support and portal access
- Examination administration
- Student services
- Campus facility maintenance

You cannot opt out of this fee. It's deducted automatically from your e-wallet or added to your required balance.

## What the NOUN Compulsory Fee Covers

The compulsory fee is best understood as a shared semester charge. It may appear in your registration or fee breakdown beside other charge lines such as course registration, examination registration, or programme-specific items.

The exact label and amount can change, so treat the list below as an explanation of what the charge commonly supports, not as an official fee schedule.

### Academic Administration
- Student registration processing
- Records management
- Verification and academic documentation support
- General administration linked to your semester record

### Library and Learning Support
- Access to learning-support services where available
- Digital or physical library administration
- Study-support infrastructure connected to your student record

### IT and Portal Operations
- Student portal access and maintenance
- Online registration workflows
- Payment and record-processing systems
- Technical support channels connected to student services

### Examination and Result Processing
- Exam administration support
- Result-processing workflows
- Academic record updates after examination activities

### Study Centre and Student Services
- General student-service administration
- Study-centre support where applicable
- Help-desk or complaint-resolution workflows

Do not use a fixed amount from an old article, screenshot, social media post, or another student's portal as your own payable amount. Your current portal breakdown is the figure that matters.

## Why is Compulsory Fee Mandatory?

NOUN charges compulsory fee because:

1. **It funds core operations** - Without it, university cannot maintain systems
2. **It's standardized** - Ensures all students pay equally for shared services
3. **It's non-refundable** - Because services are provided regardless of whether you use them
4. **It covers infrastructure** - Portals, servers, and systems benefit all students

You benefit from compulsory fee services automatically, even if you don't consciously use them.

---

## Common Misunderstandings

**"Can I skip compulsory fee?"**
No. It's mandatory. You cannot register for courses without paying it.

**"What if I don't use library or IT services?"**
You still must pay. The fee covers infrastructure available to you, even if you choose not to use it.

**"Is this on top of tuition?"**
Yes. Compulsory fee is separate from course tuition. Your total bill includes both.

**"Why is compulsory fee same for all programs?"**
Base compulsory fee is same, but total might vary if additional program-specific fees apply.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: When is compulsory fee charged?

Every semester at registration. It's typically combined with your course registration fees.

### Q2: Can compulsory fee be refunded if I withdraw?

Within first 2 weeks of enrollment, potentially yes (depends on withdrawal policy). After that, generally no.

### Q3: Is compulsory fee different for undergraduate vs postgraduate?

Base compulsory fee is same, but postgraduate might have additional research or thesis fees on top.

### Q4: Can I dispute the compulsory fee amount?

No. The amount is set by NOUN and applies uniformly. You cannot negotiate.

## Important Warnings

⚠️ **Do NOT:**
- Assume you can skip compulsory fee to save money (you can't—it's mandatory)
- Pay only course tuition without compulsory fee (payment will be rejected)
- Confuse compulsory fee with elective course fees (they're separate)
- Expect refund of compulsory fee after deadline (non-refundable)

✓ **DO:**
- Factor compulsory fee into your semester budget
- Pay it on time during registration window
- Keep proof of payment
- Verify exact amount through official portal

## Final Advice

The compulsory fee is part of the semester cost structure, but the amount should never be guessed. Before paying, open your own current registration or fee breakdown, confirm the session and semester, and save the final receipt after payment.

If the compulsory-fee line looks unusual, compare it with the official fee information available to you and ask NOUN support or your study centre before repeating payment.


## Requirements Before You Start

Before you act, prepare your matriculation number or application record, the current academic session, the relevant course code or payment reference, and a safe place to save receipts or screenshots. Do not share passwords, one-time codes, card details, or unrestricted portal access with anyone offering help.

## Step-by-Step Verification Process

1. Start from an official NOUN page or a portal link you independently verified.
2. Confirm the session, semester, programme, level, course code, or payment reference that applies to the task.
3. Read the page carefully before clicking a button that submits, pays, updates, or registers anything.
4. Save the final receipt, slip, acknowledgement, or screen record where available.
5. If the outcome is unclear, stop and prepare one clear support report instead of repeating the action.

## Common Mistakes

- Relying on another student's screenshot as your own official record.
- Paying, registering, or updating a record before checking the current session and details.
- Closing the page without saving the reference or final confirmation.
- Sharing portal credentials with a helper or agent.
- Repeating a failed-looking action before confirming whether the first attempt succeeded.

## Troubleshooting

If the portal does not show the expected result, first confirm that you are using the correct official workflow and that your internet session has not expired. Compare the current screen with your saved evidence. If a payment, registration, profile, or course record remains wrong, prepare the exact date, reference, course code, screenshot, and expected outcome before contacting official support.
