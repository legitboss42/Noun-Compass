## Quick Answer

Yes, there is now strong official evidence that NOUN students are participating in NELFUND. The clearest proof is that NOUN publicly promotes NELFUND on its homepage and published an official update on 3 March 2026 confirming approved disbursement of `N13,780,250.00` to 90 NOUN students' e-wallets.

What you should not do is stretch that evidence further than it goes. The current verified evidence supports NOUN participation. It does not fully settle every category question about postgraduate students, returning students, new-student timing, or exact JAMB regularization rules.

## What Is Officially Verified Right Now

The safest starting point is to separate evidence from interpretation.

### Verified fact 1: NOUN publicly points students to NELFUND

The official [NOUN homepage](https://nou.edu.ng/) currently includes a NELFUND call-to-action that directs students to apply. That matters because it shows institutional recognition. This is not a random forum mention or an unofficial Telegram screenshot. It is a visible signal on an official NOUN property.

### Verified fact 2: NOUN publicly announced disbursement to NOUN students

The stronger evidence came from NOUN's own announcement titled [VC approves disbursement of NELFUND to NOUN students' e-wallets](https://nou.edu.ng/vc-approves-disbursement-of-nelfund-to-noun-students-e-wallets/).

That official post confirmed:

- the announcement date was 3 March 2026
- 90 NOUN students had reached the approved disbursement stage
- the approved amount was `N13,780,250.00`
- the NOUN-side destination mentioned in the announcement was students' e-wallets

This moves the conversation beyond theory. It shows that at least some NOUN students have already passed through the real NELFUND pathway successfully.

### Verified fact 3: The public NELFUND flow checks institution, matric number, JAMB number, and date of birth

The official public registration flow at [portal.nelf.gov.ng/auth/register](https://portal.nelf.gov.ng/auth/register) verified that NELFUND currently asks for:

- institution selection
- matric number
- JAMB registration number
- date of birth

That tells us NELFUND is not treating a NOUN application like a vague manual request. It expects linked student records and JAMB-linked verification before the flow continues.

## What Is Still Unclear

This is where students need caution.

The research docs deliberately held back several claims because the official sources reviewed in Phase 7.4 did not answer them clearly.

### Postgraduate eligibility is still unverified

The reviewed official sources did not clearly state that postgraduate NOUN students are eligible. That does not prove they are excluded, but it also does not justify telling students they are definitely included.

### Returning-student eligibility is still unverified

The official sources reviewed did not separate returning students into a clearly eligible or ineligible category. So this should stay marked unverified until stronger official wording appears.

### New-student timing is still unverified

One of the most common student questions is whether a newly admitted NOUN student can apply immediately. The current research does not answer that directly. There is no verified timing statement in the reviewed official sources saying `apply immediately after admission` or `wait until a later milestone`.

### Exact JAMB regularization rules are not fully verified

This is one of the biggest confusion points in the cluster. The public NELFUND flow clearly uses JAMB-linked verification. But the reviewed official sources did not clearly prove that every NOUN student must complete the same JAMB regularization step in the same way before applying.

That is why this site should say:

- JAMB-linked verification is verified
- exact universal JAMB regularization rules for NOUN students are not yet fully verified

## How To Judge Eligibility Claims Safely

When a finance-related topic is changing quickly, students need a filtering habit.

### Trust a claim more when:

- it comes from the official NOUN website
- it comes from an official NELFUND page
- it matches the current public application workflow
- it does not promise guaranteed approval or guaranteed timing

### Trust a claim less when:

- it comes from a forwarded screenshot without a source
- it turns one student's case into a rule for everyone
- it guarantees that all NOUN students qualify
- it treats unverified regularization claims as universal facts

### Pause completely when:

- a claim asks you to pay someone to `unlock` eligibility
- a claim says approval is automatic
- a claim tells you to ignore your own official record and rely on a copied answer
