## Quick Answer

To reset your NOUN portal password:

1. Open the official student portal.
2. Click `Forgot Password`.
3. Enter your student ID and registered email.
4. Request the reset link.
5. Open the email and create a new password.
6. Return to the portal and log in again.

If the reset email does not arrive, do not keep guessing. First check spam, confirm you used the right email, and only then request another link.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide covers:

- the normal password-reset flow
- what to do if the email does not arrive
- what to do if the link expires
- how to reduce the chance of another lockout

## Step-by-Step Password Reset Process

### Step 1: Go to the login page

1. Open the official NOUN student portal.
2. Make sure the page is the real portal before you enter anything.
3. Stay on the login screen until you see the password-recovery link.

### Step 2: Click `Forgot Password`

Look for a password-recovery option near the login form. The wording may vary slightly, but it should clearly point to password reset or account recovery.

### Step 3: Enter your details

You will usually need:

- your student ID
- the email address already linked to the account

Use the same email the portal expects. If you enter a different one, the reset may not work even if the account itself is fine.

### Step 4: Check your email

After you request the reset:

1. Check your inbox.
2. Check spam or junk if nothing appears.
3. Give it a little time before you request another link.

### Step 5: Open the reset link

Open the email, use the reset link, and create a new password. If the link has expired, request a fresh one instead of trying to reuse the old email.

### Step 6: Save the new password and log in again

Once the reset succeeds:

1. Save the new password somewhere safe.
2. Return to the official portal.
3. Log in with the new password.

## Troubleshooting Password Reset Issues

### The reset email did not arrive

Check spam first. Then confirm that you used the same email already tied to your account. If both are correct and nothing still arrives, request a new link once rather than repeating the same action over and over.

### The reset link expired

Request a fresh link. That is usually faster than trying to rescue an old one.

### The account locked after repeated failed logins

Pause for a while, then use the reset flow calmly. Repeated guesses usually slow you down rather than helping.

### The new password still does not work

Check for small issues first:

- wrong student ID
- old password saved in the browser
- caps lock or typing errors
- logging in with email when the portal expects student ID

If those are fine and the portal still refuses access, prepare one clear support request instead of trying random variations.

## Preventing Future Lockouts

1. Save your password somewhere secure.
2. Avoid obvious passwords linked to your name, phone number, or student ID.
3. Use a password you are not already using elsewhere.
4. Update it when you have a reason to think it is no longer safe.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long do reset links stay active?

They are usually time-limited. If yours no longer works, request a new one.

### Can I reset my password without email access?

Not through the normal portal flow. If you can no longer access the registered email, you will likely need official help.

### My registered email is old. What should I do?

Contact the right support or study-centre channel and explain the problem clearly before you try more reset requests.

### Is it safe to reset on public Wi-Fi?

The reset process itself may still work, but it is better to handle account recovery on a device and network you trust if you have that option.

### Can I reuse my old password?

You may be able to, but it is usually better to choose a fresh one.

## Important Warnings

**Keep these basics in mind:**

- never share a reset link with another person
- never share the new password with anyone
- change the password again if you think someone else has seen it
- use a unique password instead of reusing one from another account
- log out on shared computers after using the portal

**Do not:**

- use weak passwords like `123456` or `password`
- leave a written password lying around where other people can find it
- keep guessing after several failed attempts
- hand over your email password to anyone offering help

**Do:**

- create a stronger password with letters, numbers, and symbols
- keep it in a secure password manager or another safe place
- update it if you think the account may no longer be secure

## Final Advice

Your NOUN portal access matters because it controls registration, payments, and result access. Treat it carefully.

If the first reset attempt does not work, slow down and diagnose the exact problem instead of trying five different things at once. That usually gets you back in faster.
