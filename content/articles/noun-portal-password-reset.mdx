## Quick Answer

To reset your NOUN portal password:

1. Go to the official NOUN student portal
2. Click "Forgot Password"
3. Enter your Student ID and email
4. Click "Send Reset Link"
5. Check email for reset link (may take 5-10 minutes)
6. Click link and create new password
7. Log in with new password

Total time: 10-15 minutes if email works correctly.

## Step-by-Step Password Reset Process

### Step 1: Go to the Login Page

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
2. Type the address: **the official NOUN student portal** (VERIFY the exact spelling)
3. Press Enter

Make sure you're on the official site, not a fake one.

### Step 2: Click "Forgot Password"

On the login page, look for:
- "Forgot Password?" link (usually below login button)
- "Reset Password" link
- "Can't access your account?" link

Click whichever link appears.

### Step 3: Enter Your Information

You'll see a form asking for:
- **Student ID:** Enter your 9-digit NOUN enrollment number
- **Email Address:** Enter the email registered with your account

Important: Email must match what you registered when enrolling.

Click **"Send Reset Link"**

### Step 4: Check Your Email

After 2-10 minutes, check your email for:
- Subject line: Something like "NOUN Password Reset"
- Sender: reset@the official NOUN student portal or similar
- A clickable link in the email

**Check spam/junk folder** if not in inbox (emails sometimes get filtered).

### Step 5: Click the Reset Link

1. Open the email from NOUN
2. Click the blue "Reset Password" button or link
3. You'll be taken to a password reset page

**Important:** Reset links usually expire after 24 hours. If link doesn't work, it may have expired—request a new one.

### Step 6: Create Your New Password

You'll see form with:
- **Current Password:** Leave blank (you're resetting because you forgot it)
- **New Password:** Create strong password
- **Confirm Password:** Type same password again

**Password requirements:**
- At least 8 characters
- Mix of uppercase and lowercase
- At least one number
- At least one special character (!@#$%^&*)

**Example strong password:** Noun2024@MyStudy

### Step 7: Save Your New Password

After password reset succeeds:
1. You'll see confirmation message
2. Write down your new password somewhere safe (password manager, physical notebook)
3. Close the page
4. Go back to the official NOUN student portal
5. Log in with Student ID and new password

## Troubleshooting Password Reset Issues

### Issue: "Reset Email Didn't Arrive"

**Solution 1: Check spam folder**
- Search spam/junk folder for "NOUN" or "reset"
- Mark legitimate NOUN email as "Not Spam"

**Solution 2: Wait longer**
- Email can take 5-10 minutes to arrive
- Wait 10 minutes and check again

**Solution 3: Request new reset link**
- Go back to password reset page
- Enter Student ID and email again
- Request a new link

**Solution 4: Verify correct email**
- Check what email you registered with (check your admission letter)
- Your registered email might be different than current email
- Request reset using original registration email

### Issue: "Reset Link Expired"

**Solution:** Request a new reset link. Links expire after 24 hours.

### Issue: "Account Locked After Multiple Failed Logins"

**What happened:** You tried logging in too many times with wrong password. Portal temporarily locked your account.

**Solution:**
1. Wait 30-60 minutes
2. Try reset password process
3. This should unlock account

### Issue: "I Reset Password But Can't Log In"

**Check:**
- Are you using Student ID or email to login? (Should be Student ID)
- Are you using correct new password you just set?
- Is Caps Lock on? Passwords are case-sensitive
- Try logging in on different browser

If still not working, contact NOUN IT support.

## Preventing Future Lockouts

1. **Keep password written safely** (physical notebook or password manager)
2. **Don't use obvious passwords** (birthdate, student ID, etc.)
3. **Make it unique** (not used on other websites)
4. **Change password twice yearly** (good security practice)
5. **Enable 2FA** (two-factor authentication) if available

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How long do reset links stay active?

Usually 24 hours. If you don't reset within 24 hours, request a new link.

### Q2: Can I reset my password without email access?

Not through the portal. You'd need to contact NOUN IT support directly with identification proof.

### Q3: My registered email is old and I can't access it. What do I do?

Contact NOUN student services or your study centre. They can help update your registered email before password reset.

### Q4: Is it safe to reset password on public WiFi?

Yes, the reset process is encrypted. But after resetting, avoid logging into portal on public WiFi if possible.

### Q5: Can I choose my old password again?

You can, but it's not recommended for security. Better to choose a completely new password.

## Important Warnings

⚠️ **Security Critical:**
- Never share your reset link with anyone
- Never share your new password (NOUN support will never ask for it)
- Change your password if you think someone knows it
- Use a unique password (not the same as other accounts)
- Log out on shared computers after using portal

⚠️ **Do NOT:**
- Use "123456" or "password" as your new password
- Write password on paper and leave it lying around
- Use the same password for multiple accounts
- Share your email password (attackers can reset portal password)

✓ **DO:**
- Create a strong password with letters, numbers, and symbols
- Store password in a password manager
- Change password regularly (every 6 months)
- Use 2-factor authentication if available

## Final Advice

Your NOUN portal access is valuable—it's where you register, pay fees, and access results. Protect it seriously.

A lost password is a minor inconvenience compared to account compromise. If you notice unusual activity in your account (charges you didn't make, courses registered without your action), change your password immediately and contact support.

Security is your responsibility. Treat your NOUN credentials with the same care you'd treat your bank login.
