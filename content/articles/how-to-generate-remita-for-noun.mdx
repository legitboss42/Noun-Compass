## Quick Answer

To generate Remita for NOUN payment:

1. Log into NOUN portal (the official NOUN student portal)
2. Go to "Payments" → "Pay School Fees"
3. Select what you're paying for (school fees, course materials, etc.)
4. Click "Generate Payment Reference"
5. System generates your RRR (Remita Reference Number)
6. Use this RRR to pay via Remita or bank transfer
7. Portal updates within 24–48 hours

Total time: 2 minutes.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Generating Remita for NOUN

### Step 1: Log Into Your NOUN Portal Account

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
2. Go to **the official NOUN student portal**
3. Enter your **Student ID** (example: 123456789)
4. Enter your **Password**
5. If you see a 2-factor authentication prompt, enter your **OTP** (One-Time Password sent to your phone)
6. Click **"Login"**

**Can't log in?** [See our password reset guide](/articles/noun-portal-password-reset).

### Step 2: Navigate to the Payment Section

Once logged in:

1. Look for the **Main Dashboard** or **Student Home**
2. Find the menu on the left side or top of the page
3. Click **"Payments"** or **"Pay Fees"**
4. Select **"School Fees"** or **"Pay Outstanding Balance"**

You should now see your outstanding balance displayed.

1. The system usually pre-fills the **Amount** based on what you selected
2. **Double-check this amount matches what you expect**
3. If the amount looks wrong, go back and select the correct fee
4. Click **"Proceed"** or **"Generate Payment Reference"**

### Step 5: Generate Your Remita Reference Number (RRR)

This is the key step:

1. The portal will display your **RRR** (Remita Reference Number)
2. The RRR looks like: **123456789012** (12 digits)
3. **Write down or screenshot this number immediately**
4. Also note:
   - Amount to pay (exact naira amount)
   - Recipient: "National Open University of Nigeria"
   - The RRR validity period (usually 24–48 hours)

**Important:** Your RRR is unique to this specific payment. If you need to pay a different amount later, you'll generate a new RRR.

### Step 6: Pay Via Remita Online

You now have two options:

**Option A: Pay Directly Through Remita (Easiest)**

1. Look for a **"Pay Online"** or **"Continue to Remita"** button on the NOUN portal
2. Click it—you'll be directed to remita.net
3. Verify the RRR number matches what you copied
4. Enter the amount
5. Select your bank from the dropdown
6. Click **"Pay"**
7. You'll be directed to your bank's payment page
8. Complete the transaction using your internet banking details
9. After successful payment, you'll receive a confirmation
10. **Screenshot this confirmation**

**Option B: Pay Using Bank Transfer**

1. Go to your bank's online banking portal
2. Select **"Bill Payment"** or **"Pay Utility Bills"**
3. Look for **"Remita"** or **"NOUN"** as the service provider
4. Enter:
   - **RRR:** [Your 12-digit reference number]
   - **Amount:** [The exact amount you noted]
5. Click **"Pay"** and follow your bank's verification steps
6. You'll receive an SMS confirmation from your bank
7. **Keep this SMS and screenshot**

## Troubleshooting Remita Payment Issues

### Issue: "RRR Generation Failed" Error

**Cause:** Your account has an issue or the portal is temporarily down.

**Solution:**
1. Clear your browser cache (Ctrl + Shift + Delete)
2. Log out completely
3. Wait 5 minutes
4. Log back in and try again
5. If still failing, try a different browser (Firefox if you use Chrome)
6. Contact NOUN support if it persists

### Issue: "Payment Failed at Bank" Error

**Cause:** Your bank declined the transaction (insufficient funds, wrong PIN, etc.).

**Solution:**
1. Check your bank account balance
2. Verify your internet banking details are correct
3. Try paying from a different device or browser
4. Contact your bank to ensure international/online transactions are enabled
5. Try again with the SAME RRR (within 24 hours)

### Issue: "Invalid RRR" Error at Remita

**Cause:** You entered the wrong RRR number or the number expired (>48 hours).

**Solution:**
1. Double-check you copied the RRR correctly (12 digits, no spaces)
2. If >48 hours have passed, generate a new RRR from the NOUN portal
3. Copy the new RRR carefully and try payment again

### Issue: Payment Confirmed by Bank But Not Showing in NOUN Portal

**Cause:** The bank sent payment but NOUN hasn't received it yet (typical delay: 24 hours).

**Solution:**
1. Keep your bank confirmation SMS
2. Wait until the next business day (24-48 hours)
3. Check the NOUN portal again
4. If still not updated after 48 hours, contact NOUN support with your bank's transaction reference

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Using an Expired RRR

**The Problem:** Students wait too long between generating and paying with an RRR.

**The Reality:** RRR numbers expire after 24–48 hours. An expired RRR will be rejected by Remita.

**Solution:** Generate your RRR immediately before payment, then pay within 1–2 hours. If you miss the window, generate a new RRR.

### Mistake 2: Paying Wrong Amount

**The Problem:** Manually entering wrong amount when paying (typing error).

**The Reality:** The payment goes through but for the wrong amount. You'll owe the difference.

**Solution:** Let the system pre-fill the amount whenever possible. If you must enter it manually, double-check before clicking "Pay."

### Mistake 3: Not Keeping Your Bank Confirmation

**The Problem:** Students delete their bank SMS or don't screenshot the confirmation.

**The Reality:** If the payment doesn't show up in NOUN after 48 hours, you need proof you paid.

**Solution:** Keep all bank confirmations and NOUN portal screenshots for the entire semester.

### Mistake 4: Trying to Pay with an Invalid RRR Second Time

**The Problem:** First payment failed, so student uses the same RRR again.

**The Reality:** RRR can only be used once. If it expired or wasn't successful, you need a NEW RRR.

**Solution:** Always generate a fresh RRR for each payment attempt. Never reuse an RRR.

### Mistake 5: Not Waiting for Portal to Update

**The Problem:** Students check portal 30 minutes after payment and assume it failed.

**The Reality:** NOUN portal updates every 24–48 hours. Premature checking causes unnecessary stress.

**Solution:** Wait minimum 24 hours before assuming payment failed. Check again the next day.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Is it safe to pay online via Remita?

**A:** Yes, Remita is a trusted payment gateway used by thousands of Nigerian government agencies. Your payment is:
- Encrypted during transmission
- Protected by bank-level security
- Processed through licensed financial institutions
- Traceable (you have an RRR to reference)

### Q2: What if I accidentally generate multiple RRRs?

**A:** Each RRR is unique and you only pay once. If you generated 5 RRRs, you have 5 payment options but are only obligated to pay one. Just select whichever RRR you use and ignore the others.

### Q3: Can I pay someone else's fees using their RRR?

**A:** Technically yes, but it's not recommended. The payment gets credited to their portal. If there's a dispute, you might not be able to recover it. It's better for each person to pay their own fees.

### Q4: What if my internet cuts out during payment?

**A:** Your bank might still process the payment even if your screen disconnected. Don't pay again immediately. Wait a few minutes, then check your bank statement. If you were charged, wait for NOUN portal to update (24-48 hours).

### Q5: Can I get a refund if I paid too much?

**A:** This page does not verify a universal refund timetable or outcome. If you paid the wrong amount or created an overpayment, preserve the reference, receipt, and current portal status, then confirm the current official refund process before assuming how quickly it will be resolved.

### Q6: Do I need to pay with Remita or can I use other methods?

**A:** Remita is the primary method NOUN accepts. Some study centres might accept:
- Bank transfer directly to NOUN
- Cash deposits at study centre (rare)
- Checks (rare)

Always check with your study centre for alternatives.

### Q7: How long does bank processing take?

**A:** Typically:
- Online payment via bank portal: 2-4 hours
- Online payment via Remita: 2-4 hours
- Bank transfer: 24 hours
- Study centre cash deposit: immediate but must wait for NOUN to process (24-48 hours)

### Q8: Can I pay for multiple semesters at once?

**A:** Usually no. NOUN generates separate RRRs for each semester. You'll need to make separate payments. Contact your study centre if you want to pay multiple semesters simultaneously.
