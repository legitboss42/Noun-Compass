## Quick Answer

To generate Remita for a NOUN payment:

1. Log into the official student portal.
2. Open `Payments`.
3. Choose the fee you want to pay.
4. Generate the payment reference.
5. Save the RRR immediately.
6. Complete the payment through the approved Remita route.
7. Return later to confirm that the portal has updated.

The job is usually quick. The part that causes trouble is not generating the RRR. It is paying the wrong one, paying twice, or assuming a normal update delay means the payment failed.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide covers:

- where to open the payment section
- how to generate the correct RRR
- how to complete the payment safely
- what to save for your records
- what to do if the portal does not update right away

## Why This Matters

Most students only need to get three things right here: generate the correct reference, pay it once, and keep the right evidence. Problems usually start when someone uses an old RRR, guesses the amount, or repeats the payment before checking the first one properly.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Generating Remita for NOUN

### Step 1: Log into your NOUN portal account

1. Open the official NOUN student portal.
2. Enter your student ID and password.
3. Finish any extra verification the portal asks for.

If you cannot log in, use the [password reset guide](/articles/noun-portal-password-reset) before you try to generate a payment reference.

### Step 2: Open the payment section

Once you are inside the portal:

1. Look for `Payments`, `Pay Fees`, or the bursary area.
2. Choose the payment type you want to handle.
3. Check the page carefully before moving on.

1. Review the amount shown on the page.
2. Compare it with your current portal record.
3. If it looks wrong, stop and recheck before you continue.

### Step 5: Generate your Remita Reference Number

Once the details are correct:

1. Click the button that generates the payment reference.
2. Save the RRR immediately.
3. Save the amount and the payment purpose with it.

Keep in mind that an RRR is tied to that specific payment attempt. If you need to pay for something else later, you may need a different reference.

### Step 6: Pay through the approved Remita route

You can usually continue from the portal into Remita or pay through a bank route that accepts the RRR.

**If you pay online:**

1. Continue to the Remita payment page.
2. Confirm the RRR and amount again.
3. Complete the payment once.
4. Save the final confirmation.

**If you pay through a bank route:**

1. Use the exact RRR you generated.
2. Enter the correct amount.
3. Keep the bank receipt, SMS, or transaction evidence.

## Troubleshooting Remita Payment Issues

### RRR generation failed

Clear the page, log out, and try again after a short pause. If the same issue keeps repeating, compare the payment type and account state before you assume the portal is simply down.

### Payment failed at bank level

That usually points to the bank side rather than the portal side. Check your balance, the payment details, and the status from your bank before you generate another reference.

### Remita says the RRR is invalid

Recheck the number carefully. If the reference is old or no longer usable, generate a fresh one from the portal instead of forcing the old one again.

### The bank confirmed payment but the portal has not updated

Do not pay again immediately. Save the transaction evidence, wait, then check the status again through the official portal before you escalate.

## Common Mistakes

### Using an old RRR

Students sometimes return to a saved reference and assume it is still fine. If the reference is no longer valid, generate a new one from the current portal page.

### Paying the wrong amount

One careless figure can turn a simple payment into a support issue. Always compare the amount on the portal with what you are about to submit.

### Failing to keep proof

If the portal does not update, your bank confirmation and payment record become important. Save them while they are easy to find.

### Paying twice too quickly

This is one of the easiest ways to create confusion. If the first payment is still being processed, a second payment can make the situation worse instead of better.

## Important Warnings

**Do not:**

- pay through an unofficial helper or random payment link
- reuse a doubtful or outdated RRR without checking it
- guess the amount when the portal is showing something else
- repeat the payment before you confirm what happened to the first one

**Do:**

- generate the RRR from the official NOUN portal
- save the RRR and payment evidence immediately
- wait and recheck the portal before you panic
- prepare one clear support report if the issue remains unresolved

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Remita safe for NOUN payments?

It is the normal payment route students expect to see in this workflow. What matters most is that you start from the official NOUN portal and confirm each step before you submit the payment.

### What if I generated more than one RRR?

Check which one you actually used. Do not assume every generated reference should be paid.

### What if I paid but the portal has not updated?

Save the transaction evidence, give the system time, and recheck the official portal before you decide the payment failed.

### Can I get a refund if I paid too much?

This page does not verify a universal refund timetable or outcome. If you overpaid, preserve the reference, receipt, and current portal status, then confirm the current official process before assuming how quickly it will be resolved.
