## Which Ogun Centres Were Confirmed?

The 19 June 2026 Ogun review found **two fully verified centres**:

- `Abeokuta Study Centre Ogun`
- `Awa-Ijebu Community Study Centre Ogun`

The audit also found `Ilaro Community Study Centre Ogun` as **partially verified** and `Abeokuta Correctional Service Study Centre Ogun` as **unverified**. So if you need a safer Ogun centre page, the right move is to begin with Abeokuta or Awa-Ijebu, not with every Ogun-linked name you may see online.

## Why Ogun Earned Its Own Page

Ogun warranted a dedicated page for three practical reasons:

- `Abeokuta Study Centre Ogun` had direct verification support
- the state had enough surrounding centre signals to justify a regional page
- students are likely to search by state or city, especially around Abeokuta

At the same time, the audit specifically recommended **not** creating a standalone Abeokuta-only article first. A broader Ogun page made more sense because the state has more than one meaningful centre signal.

## Ogun Has Two Strong Anchors, Not One

A lot of students will search only for `Abeokuta Study Centre` because Abeokuta is the most obvious Ogun location in the audited data. That is understandable, but the research pass showed something more helpful: Ogun authority is stronger when students also understand `Awa-Ijebu Community Study Centre Ogun` as part of the same trustworthy cluster.

That matters for two reasons:

- it prevents the state page from collapsing into one-city thinking
- it gives students a better way to compare proximity with evidence quality

In practice, this means the safest Ogun strategy is:

- start with Abeokuta when you want the strongest anchor
- consider Awa-Ijebu when geography makes it more practical
- treat Ilaro as a double-check case, not a first-choice confirmed row

## What To Confirm Before Using Ilaro

If Ilaro is your likely route, verify:

- whether the current centre detail still appears consistently in official signals
- whether your task truly requires a centre visit
- whether a stronger verified Ogun centre should be your first contact point instead

This matters most for students who are travelling because of a result, support, or registration problem that might already have a cleaner online path.

## Practical Ogun Questions To Ask Yourself

Before you travel, ask:

- Am I choosing the nearest centre or the best-verified centre?
- Is my issue admission, registration, TMA, exams, results, or support?
- Do I already have the reference or evidence I will need at the centre?
- If the first centre redirects me, do I know the next workflow to use?

Those questions are what turn a centre page from a directory into a useful student guide.

## Common Ogun Mistakes To Avoid

- treating `Abeokuta` and `Ilaro` as equally confirmed
- assuming a correctional-centre row is automatically usable as general student guidance
- using a copied third-party address without checking it against current official signals
- travelling to the nearest-sounding centre instead of the strongest verified one
