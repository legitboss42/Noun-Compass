## What Changes A Returning Student's Bill?

Returning students should estimate fees from their current programme, semester, intended course load, outstanding courses, and examination choices. Use the estimate to plan, then confirm the final payable amount through the current official portal before payment.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide explains why returning-student totals change and how to build a cleaner semester budget from the records that apply to your own account.

## Requirements Before You Start

Build this semester's estimate from your programme, intended courses, carryovers, and examination choices. Keep the generated bill and receipts together, and redact credentials from any evidence you send.

## Step-by-Step Budget Plan

1. Confirm the current session, semester, programme, and course level before estimating any charge.
2. Separate compulsory semester charges from course-registration and exam-registration charges.
3. Add carryovers, repeat courses, or intended electives only after confirming they truly belong in the current plan.
4. Compare the estimate with the current official portal bill before funding the wallet or making payment.
5. Save the final bill, receipt, and related references in one place before moving into course or exam registration.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Funding the wallet without first checking whether the charge belongs to the current semester.
- Mixing school-fee, course, and exam costs together until the final record becomes hard to verify.
- Repeating a payment because the portal is slow instead of confirming the first transaction status.
- Relying on another student's semester pattern without matching your own level, carryovers, and course plan.

## When The Semester Estimate Does Not Match

### Your total changed from last semester

Compare current course load, outstanding courses, examination choices, and charge descriptions. A previous semester is useful for budgeting but is not proof of the current payable amount.

### You have carryover or repeat courses

Review the official result and registration guidance before estimating. Outstanding courses can affect the course list, workload, and related charges.

### You are considering electives

Estimate only electives you are eligible and intend to register. An available elective should not be added merely to match another student's course list. If you still need to separate compulsory courses from optional ones, review [What is the Difference Between Core Courses and Electives at NOUN?](/articles/noun-core-courses-vs-electives) before final registration.

### The fee checker differs from your portal

The checker is there to help you plan. Your current portal bill still takes priority when it is time to pay.

### You want to fund the wallet before choosing courses

Build a careful estimate and keep a reasonable buffer, but avoid treating the estimate as a final invoice. Confirm the official workflow and current charges.

### A completed payment did not reflect

Do not immediately repeat the transaction. Keep the reference, receipt, amount, date, and current status, then use the official support route with one clear timeline.

## Important Warnings

- Begin with the courses and exams you plan to register this semester.
- Include unresolved carryover courses only after confirming how they appear in your record.
- Compare your estimate with the itemised amount in the current portal.
- Save the registration list, payment references, and receipts for the semester.
- Check a delayed wallet entry before making another payment.
