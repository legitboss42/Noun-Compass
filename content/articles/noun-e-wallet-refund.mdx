## Quick Answer

If you believe your NOUN e-wallet needs a refund, start by confirming the exact transaction, saving the payment evidence, and checking the current official portal or support route. This page does not treat refund approval, refund timing, or refund eligibility as automatic.

## What This Guide Covers

This guide explains:

- how to confirm whether the problem is really a refund issue
- what evidence to collect before escalating
- why students should avoid guessing about timelines or outcomes
- how to prepare a clear support request around an e-wallet dispute

## Start By Confirming The Real Problem

Students often use the word `refund` for several different problems:

- the wallet did not reflect after payment
- the amount reflected incorrectly
- the wrong charge was paid
- the same transaction seems to appear twice
- the student now wants money returned after a change in plan

Those are not automatically the same workflow. Before you ask for a refund, identify which of them actually happened on your record.

## Safer Step-By-Step Refund Preparation

### Step 1: Confirm the transaction in your own record

Open the current official portal and compare:

- the transaction reference or RRR
- the wallet or payment-history entry
- the date and amount
- the current status shown in the system

Use [How to pay NOUN school fees safely](/articles/how-to-pay-noun-school-fees) and [How to view your NOUN financial statement](/articles/noun-financial-statement) if you first need to reconstruct what happened.

### Step 2: Save the minimum evidence

Prepare:

- the payment reference or RRR
- the receipt or bank debit proof
- the visible portal status
- a short note explaining what you expected and what actually happened

If the issue involves duplicate or unclear charges, save both records rather than describing them from memory.

### Step 3: Decide whether this is a reflection problem or a refund problem

Not every unresolved payment should immediately be framed as a refund request. Sometimes the safer first question is whether the payment is still processing, mismatched, or attached to the wrong charge line.

That distinction matters because a premature refund request can make the support conversation less clear.

### Step 4: Use the current official route

Once the issue is clearly documented, use the official NOUN support or portal route that currently handles the case. Keep the acknowledgement or ticket ID and avoid opening duplicate complaints for the same payment trail.

Use [NOUN Support Ticket Guide](/articles/noun-support-ticket-guide) to prepare the message correctly.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know whether I should ask for a refund or a payment investigation?

If the money left your bank but the portal status is still unclear, start by confirming the transaction path and current status first. A payment investigation and a refund request are not always the same thing.

### What evidence should I keep?

Keep the payment reference, receipt, visible portal status, and a short timeline of what happened. These matter more than a long emotional complaint.

### How long does a refund take?

This page does not verify a universal refund timetable. Treat any fixed timeline claim as unverified unless your current official route states it clearly.

### Does every overpayment automatically mean a refund?

Do not assume that. First confirm how the overpayment appears in the current official record and whether the system is treating it as a balance issue, wallet issue, or separate refund issue.

## Important Warnings

- Do not assume a refund is approved just because the payment felt wrong.
- Do not open multiple complaints for the same transaction.
- Do not guess the outcome from another student's case.
- Do not share card details, passwords, or one-time codes while trying to resolve a refund issue.
