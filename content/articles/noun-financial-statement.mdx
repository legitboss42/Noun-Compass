## Quick Answer

To view your NOUN financial statement:

1. Log into NOUN portal (the official NOUN student portal)
2. Go to "My Profile" → "Financial Statement" (or "Account Balance")
3. Download PDF or view online
4. Review balances, transactions, and payment history

Financial statement shows your complete financial record with NOUN.

## Step-by-Step: Accessing Your Financial Statement

### Step 1: Log Into NOUN Portal

1. Go to the official NOUN student portal
2. Enter Student ID
3. Enter Password
4. Complete 2FA if prompted

### Step 2: Navigate to Financial Statement

From dashboard:
1. Click "My Profile" (top right)
2. Select "Financial Information" or "Payments"
3. Look for "Financial Statement" or "Account Statement"
4. Click "View" or "Download"

### Step 3: Review Statement Contents

Your statement typically includes:

**Header Information:**
- Your name and student ID
- Statement date range
- Account status

**Account Balance Section:**
- Current balance
- Outstanding balance (amount owed)
- Credit balance (if overpaid)

**Transaction History:**
- Payments made
- Fees charged
- Adjustments or reversals where they appear in the current record
- Dates and amounts

## Understanding Your Financial Statement

### Current Balance: Understanding What You See

**Balance shows:**
- Zero balance: the portal is not showing an amount currently owed
- Positive balance: the portal is showing an amount you still need to settle
- Negative or credit balance: the portal may be showing an overpayment or unused wallet credit

**What "owing" means:** Amount deducted from e-wallet before next registration.

### Transaction History Section

Each transaction shows:
- **Date:** When transaction occurred
- **Description:** What was charged or paid (e.g., "School Fees," "Course Materials")
- **Amount:** Naira amount
- **Type:** "Charge" (fee) or "Payment" (money in)
- **Running Balance:** Your balance after that transaction

### Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: My statement shows a balance, but I paid. Why?

Processing delay. Wait 24–48 hours, then refresh statement.

### Q2: Can I download my statement?

Yes, usually there's a "Download PDF" button. This gives you an official record for records.

### Q3: What if I see fees I didn't authorize?

Contact your department immediately. Provide screenshot and transaction ID.

### Q4: My balance looks wrong. Who do I contact?

Email NOUN finance or submit support ticket in portal with your evidence.

## Important Warnings

⚠️ **Do NOT:**
- Share your financial statement with anyone outside NOUN
- Use outdated financial statements for official purposes
- Ignore discrepancies (they could affect your graduation clearance)
- Assume small charges will "go away" (they won't)

⚠️ **Verify:**
- Download fresh statement directly from portal (not cached versions)
- Compare multiple payment receipts with the statement
- Check for duplicate charges or unexplained fees
- Confirm total matches your actual payments

---

## Final Advice

Your financial statement is your transaction history and proof of payments. Treat it like a bank statement—review it regularly to catch errors early.

Keeping a personal copy of your payment receipts helps you verify the statement's accuracy. If discrepancies exist, report them quickly before they escalate or affect your graduation.

Download and save a copy of your financial statement at the end of each semester. This archive helps with future references and provides backup documentation.

## Requirements Before You Start

Before you act, prepare your matriculation number or application record, the current academic session, the relevant course code or payment reference, and a safe place to save receipts or screenshots. Do not share passwords, one-time codes, card details, or unrestricted portal access with anyone offering help.

## Common Mistakes

- Relying on another student's screenshot as your own official record.
- Paying, registering, or updating a record before checking the current session and details.
- Closing the page without saving the reference or final confirmation.
- Sharing portal credentials with a helper or agent.
- Repeating a failed-looking action before confirming whether the first attempt succeeded.

## Troubleshooting

If the portal does not show the expected result, first confirm that you are using the correct official workflow and that your internet session has not expired. Compare the current screen with your saved evidence. If a payment, registration, profile, or course record remains wrong, prepare the exact date, reference, course code, screenshot, and expected outcome before contacting official support.
