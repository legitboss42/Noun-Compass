## Quick Answer

The verified session did not expose CGPA on the first `My progress` page. It exposed CGPA, current class of degree, total credit earned, and outstanding credit on the ERP statement-of-result page opened through the Support portal's `Result statement` action. If you need these metrics, that is the page you should inspect.

## Why This Page Matters

Students often mix up three different result questions:

- where can I see grades?
- where can I see CGPA?
- where can I understand what I still owe academically?

The live session showed that these answers do not all come from the same surface. `My progress` was useful for result history, but the deeper ERP statement page was where broader academic metrics became visible.

## The Verified Path To These Metrics

The observed route was:

1. student portal
2. `My progress`
3. `Support`
4. Support portal login
5. `Result statement`
6. ERP page at `erp.nou.edu.ng/stsor`

That ERP page is the verified place to check:

- `CGPA`
- `Current class of degree`
- `Total credit earned`
- `Outstanding credit`

## How To Read These Metrics Carefully

### CGPA

In this research phase, the important verified fact is location: CGPA was visible on the ERP statement page. This guide does not attempt to re-teach a formula that was not derived from the live portal session. Instead, it helps you identify where the official value was shown.

Use CGPA here as the confirmed official metric to review, save, and compare over time.

### Current class of degree

This value appeared alongside the broader academic standing information on the same ERP page. Its importance is practical: many students look for overall academic standing and mistakenly expect the first portal page to provide it.

The verified workflow says to look deeper.

### Total credit earned

This metric helps you understand how much confirmed academic credit the system is already counting in your record.

### Outstanding credit

This metric matters because it shows what remains from the system's perspective. It is not the same thing as inventing your own estimate from memory or from informal advice.

## Why Students Misread These Fields

There are three common mistakes:

- expecting CGPA on `My progress`
- treating one incomplete result row as a full academic summary
- assuming outstanding credit and outstanding courses mean exactly the same thing

The verified workflow helps separate them:

- `My progress` gives first-layer history
- the ERP statement page gives the fuller academic summary
- outstanding courses appear in a separate section on the same ERP page
