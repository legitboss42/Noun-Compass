## The Observed Route From Course Page To Grades

In the session reviewed on 19 June 2026, the useful route started on the dashboard or course page, continued through the course `TMA` section, and ended in `Grades`. Read the `Opened`, `Closed`, and `Attempts allowed` values on your own activity before proceeding.

Use this order:

1. Open `Dashboard`.
2. Use `Recently accessed items` or open the course page.
3. Scroll to the course `TMA` section.
4. Open `TMA1`, `TMA2`, or `TMA3`.
5. Check the attempt rules before doing anything.
6. After submission, confirm the finished state and then check `Grades`.

## How To Find Your Course Before You Find The TMA

In the observed session, `My courses` was visible but not the easiest place to begin. Its controls loaded, yet the enrolled course list was not as obvious as the dashboard and direct course view. That matters because many students will naturally assume `My courses` is always the simplest route.

The more reliable workflow from the observed account was:

1. open the dashboard
2. use a direct recent link if one is available
3. otherwise open the course page itself
4. move through the course sections until you reach `TMA`

The course page for `Numerical Analysis (MTH213_261)` was long and section-heavy. It included:

- `General`
- `Ensuring Academic Integrity: Your Path to True Success`
- `Meet Your Facilitator`
- `Introduction`
- `Virtual Library`
- `Week 1` through `Week 6`
- `Revision`
- `TMA`
- `Course Feedback`

That structure tells us two useful things. First, TMAs may sit well below the opening sections. Second, students who stop at the first visible week or discussion forum can easily miss the dedicated `TMA` block completely.

## The Real TMA Entry Paths We Saw

The live session revealed three real paths into a TMA:

1. `Dashboard` -> `Recently accessed items` -> direct TMA link
2. `Course page` -> `TMA` section -> choose `TMA1`, `TMA2`, or `TMA3`
3. `TMA activity page` -> `Course index` -> `TMA` subsection -> switch between TMAs

On the observed course page, the dedicated TMA block listed all three assignments clearly.

## What To Check On The TMA Page Before You Act

Once a TMA activity page opened, the most important details were in the activity header and summary area. The observed page showed:

- `Opened`
- `Closed`
- `Attempts allowed: 1`
- the instruction `Click on Attempt TMA Now to begin the TMA`

The observed `TMA1` page displayed:

- Opened: `Monday, 2 March 2026, 12:00 AM`
- Closed: `Sunday, 7 June 2026, 11:59 PM`

Those exact dates belong to the observed course and TMA only. The lesson is not to copy the date. The lesson is to train yourself to look for the `Opened` and `Closed` fields on your own activity page before you rush into the task.

## What We Could And Could Not Observe About Submission

The live session did not expose a brand-new unattempted TMA form because the observed TMAs were already finished. That is an important limit, and it should shape how you use this page.

What we could confirm directly:

- the page uses an `Attempt TMA Now` path when available
- the observed TMAs allowed one attempt
- after completion, the page showed a finished attempt summary
- the finished state showed `Status`, `Started`, `Completed`, `Duration`, and `Review not permitted`

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

The observed platform made these mistakes especially likely:

- relying on `My courses` even when it is not the clearest route
- stopping near the top of the course page and never reaching the `TMA` section
- reading `Attempt TMA Now` but missing the finished-state summary below it
- forgetting that `Attempts allowed: 1` means the task should not be rushed
- looking only at the TMA page when the mark is actually clearer in `Grades`
- contacting support without the course code, TMA name, or safe screenshots

If you want the mistake list turned into a full prevention checklist, read [Common TMA Mistakes Students Make](/articles/common-noun-tma-mistakes).

## Troubleshooting The eLearn To TMA To Grades Journey

### The course page opens but you cannot find the TMA section

Return to the dashboard, confirm you are in the correct course, then scroll the course structure carefully. In the observed course, the TMA section came after `Revision`.

### The dashboard is visible but you do not trust the shortcut

Use the course page as the second verification route. Do not depend only on a recent item if the course name or TMA label feels unclear.

### The TMA page opens but the score is not obvious

Open `Grades`, then check the course-specific grade report. That was the clearest score surface in the live session.

### The page looks finished and you are not sure whether the TMA really submitted

Look for the attempt summary fields: `Status`, `Started`, `Completed`, and `Duration`. Those were the strongest post-submission indicators in the observed workflow.

### The workflow still does not make sense

Prepare a clean support request with the course code, TMA label, safe screenshots, and the exact page where the confusion starts. Then use the official support route shown on eLearn or the preparation steps in [NOUN Support Ticket Guide](/articles/noun-support-ticket-guide).

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Where is the fastest way to find a TMA?

In the observed session, the fastest route was often `Dashboard` -> `Recently accessed items`. The safer verification route was the course page's dedicated `TMA` section.

### How many TMAs should I expect?

The observed `Numerical Analysis (MTH213_261)` course showed `TMA1`, `TMA2`, and `TMA3`. Confirm your own course page before assuming every course matches that pattern.

### Where should I check after submission?

First check the TMA page for a finished attempt summary, then check the broader `Grades` area and the course-specific grade report.

### What if I cannot see a score?

Confirm that you are checking the right course and the grade report, not only the TMA page. If the score still is not visible, prepare safe evidence and use the official support route.

### Can NOUN Compass submit a TMA for me?

No. NOUN Compass is an independent guide site. We explain observed workflows, but we do not access student accounts, submit coursework, or change official records.
