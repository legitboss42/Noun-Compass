## Quick Answer

Carryover courses are courses you must retake (usually because you didn't pass the first time).

To register:
1. Log into NOUN portal
2. Go to "Course Registration"
3. Carryover courses appear in a separate section
4. Select carryover courses needed
5. Proceed to payment (same fee as regular courses)
6. Verify registration completed

---


## What This Guide Covers

This guide explains how to identify carryover courses, compare them with your result record, manage credit-unit limits, and avoid wrong-course registration.


## What Are Carryover Courses?

Carryover courses are courses you must register for again because:
- You failed and need to retake
- You got incomplete grade
- You didn't sit the exam
- Department requires retake

**Important:** Carryover courses have the same fees as regular courses.

## How to Register Carryover Courses

### Step 1: Log Into Portal

1. Go to the official NOUN student portal
2. Enter Student ID
3. Enter Password
4. Complete 2FA if prompted

### Step 2: Find Course Registration

1. Click **"Courses"** in left menu
2. Select **"Register Courses"** or **"Course Registration"**
3. You'll see current course options

### Step 3: Locate Carryover Section

On registration page, you should see sections:
- **Core Courses:** Required courses for your program
- **Elective Courses:** Optional courses
- **Carryover Courses:** Courses you're retaking

Click the **"Carryover Courses"** section to expand it.

### Step 4: Select Carryover Courses

1. Find the course you need to retake
2. Check the checkbox next to it
3. Course appears in your selected list
4. Repeat for all carryover courses needed

**Maximum:** Check with your department for maximum carryover courses allowed per semester (usually 2-4).

### Step 5: Proceed to Payment

1. Review all selected courses
2. Click **"Proceed to Payment"** or **"Continue"**
3. System calculates total fees
4. Verify amount includes all courses
5. Click **"Generate Payment Reference"**

### Step 6: Complete Payment

1. System generates RRR (payment reference)
2. Screenshot the RRR
3. Use RRR to pay via Remita or bank transfer
4. Wait for portal to update (24-48 hours)

### Step 7: Verify Registration

1. Wait 24 hours after payment
2. Go back to course registration
3. Verify carryover courses appear as registered
4. Download registration slip as proof

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Forgetting to Select Carryover Courses

Students accidentally register only regular courses, forgetting carryover courses. Then they can't repeat the course they need to pass.

**Solution:** Carefully check the Carryover section. Don't skip it.

### Mistake 2: Exceeding Course Limit

Students try to register too many carryover courses at once.

**Solution:** Check your department's policy on maximum courses per semester (usually 3-4 total).

### Mistake 3: Registering Same Course Twice

Accidentally selecting a course in both regular and carryover sections.

**Solution:** Review selected courses before paying. Only select each course once.

### Mistake 4: Missing Carryover Deadline

Carryover registration happens during normal registration period, not extended.

**Solution:** Pay attention to registration deadline. It's same for all courses.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Do carryover courses cost extra?

No, same fee as regular courses.

### Q2: Can I register all my failed courses at once?

Usually yes, but most departments limit how many you can take per semester (typically 2-4).

### Q3: What if I already passed a course but want to retake for higher grade?

Most universities don't allow this. Check your department's policy.

### Q4: Does a carryover course count differently on my transcript?

It appears on transcript. Your previous attempt usually still shows but the new grade replaces it (in most cases).

## Important Warnings

Do not:
- Register too many carryover courses in one semester.
- Assume you'll automatically pass just because you're retaking
- Register for carryover courses you didn't actually fail (registration will be rejected)
- Skip studying for carryover courses (failing twice is worse)

Important:
- Carryover registration has strict deadlines, and missing the window can delay your progress.
- Some departments require department permission to register carryover
- You may need to meet with an academic advisor first

Do:
- Check your failed courses list before trying to register carryover
- Confirm your department allows carryover registration
- Register during the carryover window (not during regular registration)
- Verify the course still exists and hasn't been replaced

---

## Final Advice

Carryover courses are your chance to improve your grades and academic standing. Treat them seriously.

Before registering, ask yourself: "Am I ready to pass this course?" If not, wait until next semester when you've had time to prepare. Registering when you're not ready just adds stress and wastes money.

Study groups with classmates retaking the same course can help. You're not alone—many students repeat courses. Focus on doing it right this time.
