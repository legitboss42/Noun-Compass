## Start with unresolved courses on your result

Students often use `carryover` for a course that still appears unresolved on their academic record. Confirm the exact course and status on your result statement before trying to register it again.

To register:
1. Log into NOUN portal
2. Go to "Course Registration"
3. Compare the available courses with your result statement and curriculum
4. Select only the unresolved course the current portal permits
5. Review the units and charge before submitting
6. Save the completed registration record

---


## What To Confirm Before Registration

This guide explains how to identify carryover courses, compare them with your result record, manage credit-unit limits, and avoid wrong-course registration.


## What Are Carryover Courses?

A course can remain unresolved for different reasons, including a failed grade, an incomplete result, a missed examination, or a curriculum change. The correct next step depends on what your result statement and current programme guidance show. This page does not assume one fee or registration rule applies to every course.

## How to Register Carryover Courses

### Step 1: Log Into Portal

1. Go to the official NOUN student portal
2. Enter Student ID
3. Enter Password
4. Complete 2FA if prompted

### Step 2: Find Course Registration

1. Open the current course-registration area
2. Read the labels shown on your account
3. Compare the available options with your records

### Step 3: Locate Carryover Section

The registration page may separate courses into sections such as:
- **Core Courses:** Required courses for your program
- **Elective Courses:** Optional courses
- **Carryover Courses:** Courses you're retaking

If a carryover section is visible, open it and compare every course code with your result statement. If it is not visible, do not substitute another course just to continue.

### Step 4: Select Carryover Courses

1. Find the course you need to retake
2. Check the checkbox next to it
3. Course appears in your selected list
4. Repeat only for courses you have confirmed as unresolved

Add the units for the full selection and compare them with the current credit limit shown by the portal or programme guidance.

### Step 5: Proceed to Payment

1. Review all selected courses
2. Click **"Proceed to Payment"** or **"Continue"**
3. System calculates total fees
4. Verify amount includes all courses
5. Click **"Generate Payment Reference"**

### Step 6: Complete Payment

1. System generates RRR (payment reference)
2. Screenshot the RRR
3. Use RRR to pay via Remita or bank transfer
4. Keep the payment status and reference shown by the portal

### Step 7: Verify Registration

1. Return to course registration after the payment status is confirmed
2. Open the submitted course list
3. Verify carryover courses appear as registered
4. Download registration slip as proof

## Registration Checks Students Commonly Miss

### Mistake 1: Forgetting to Select Carryover Courses

Compare the selected list with the unresolved courses on your result statement. A course is not confirmed just because it appears under a particular portal heading.

### Mistake 2: Exceeding Course Limit

Add the units for regular, elective, and carryover courses before submitting. Follow the current limit attached to your programme or portal.

### Mistake 3: Registering Same Course Twice

Accidentally selecting a course in both regular and carryover sections.

**Solution:** Review selected courses before paying. Only select each course once.

### Mistake 4: Missing Carryover Deadline

Use the current registration notice for deadlines. This page does not assume every course has the same window.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Do carryover courses cost extra?

Use the amount generated for the course on your own portal; this page does not verify one carryover fee for every programme.

### Q2: Can I register all my failed courses at once?

Register only the unresolved courses that fit the current credit limit and are available to your record.

### Q3: What if I already passed a course but want to retake for higher grade?

Do not infer NOUN's rule from other universities. Ask the current official academic-support route before registering a passed course again.

### Q4: Does a carryover course count differently on my transcript?

This page does not verify how repeated attempts are displayed or calculated. Use your current result statement and official academic guidance.

## Important Warnings

Do not:
- Register too many carryover courses in one semester.
- Assume you'll automatically pass just because you're retaking
- Register a course as carryover without confirming its status
- Skip studying for carryover courses (failing twice is worse)

Important:
- Current registration deadlines still apply.
- Some cases may require official academic clarification before the portal permits registration.

Do:
- Check your failed courses list before trying to register carryover
- Confirm the course status and current registration route
- Follow the registration window published for your session
- Verify the course still exists and hasn't been replaced

## Confirm the portal will accept the course

Start with the official result statement, not memory. Match each unresolved course to the current curriculum, check the combined credit load, and save the final registration slip. If the portal and your records disagree, document the mismatch before choosing a substitute or paying.

Study groups with classmates retaking the same course can help. You're not alone—many students repeat courses. Focus on doing it right this time.

## Requirements Before You Start

Use the result statement, current curriculum, proposed course list, and credit-unit total before selecting a carryover. Keep account and payment credentials private.

## Step-by-Step Verification Process

1. Start with the unresolved course list on your result statement.
2. Match each code to the current curriculum and semester.
3. Add the units for regular, elective, and carryover selections.
4. Save the final registration slip after submission.
5. Report any course the portal omits or labels differently before choosing a substitute.

## Troubleshooting

If the portal does not show what you expected, first confirm that you are on the right page and that your session has not expired. Compare the live screen with your saved evidence. If a payment, registration, profile, or course record still looks wrong, gather the exact date, reference, course code, screenshot, and expected outcome before you contact support.
