## Quick Answer

In the observed session, opening `See course registration slip` did not load a slip record. It showed this blocker instead: `Please register for the current semester on your home page. See procedure 'K' in 'How to do things' for steps to follow`. The safest interpretation is that registration-history proof on this account was gated by current-semester registration status.

## Why This Matters In A Results Cluster

At first glance, this looks like a registration-only issue. In practice, it also affects result troubleshooting.

Students often use registration slips to answer questions like:

- was the course really registered?
- do I have proof for this semester?
- can I compare result gaps against my registration record?

So when the registration-slip route is blocked, it becomes part of the wider academic-record workflow.

## What Was Actually Observed

The route examined was:

- `see_course_registration_slip`

The page did not show a normal registration-slip record. It showed only the blocker message telling the student to register the current semester first.

This gives us one clear, factual conclusion: on the observed account, registration-slip access depended on current-semester registration state.

## What This Blocker Probably Means Without Overclaiming

We should stay inside what the portal itself told us. The safest interpretation is:

- the system wants current-semester registration completed first
- until that condition is met, slip access may be blocked

What we should not claim:

- that this rule behaves identically for every student account
- that the blocker automatically explains every missing result
- that there is no other registration-history route elsewhere

Those points were not verified in this session.

## When Students Usually Encounter This Problem

This blocker becomes especially frustrating when a student is trying to:

- verify whether a missing result connects to registration status
- confirm the exact course history before opening a complaint
- gather evidence for Support or e-Ticketing

In other words, the blocker often appears during a results investigation, not only during routine registration review.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- assuming the blocked slip automatically means your result is invalid
- ignoring the blocker and opening duplicate complaints without evidence
- treating a friend's experience as proof your portal should behave the same way
- trying to force a conclusion before checking the main result statement page

## Troubleshooting

### I need the registration slip to investigate my result

Record the blocker, review your current-semester registration status, and use the main result workflow in parallel so you do not stop the whole investigation.

### I only want to know whether my result is available

Do not wait for the slip route to work before checking `My progress` and the Support-portal result statement.

### I think the blocker means my registration failed

That may or may not be true. This session only verified the blocker message, not the full cause across all accounts.
