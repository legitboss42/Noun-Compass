## Quick Answer

The verified route to the strongest NOUN result page was not inside the first student-portal result screen. In the observed session, the student used `Support`, moved into the separate Support portal at `support.nou.edu.ng`, logged in, and then clicked `Result statement`. That opened the ERP result page at `erp.nou.edu.ng/stsor`.

## Why This Page Matters

Many students stop at `My progress` and assume that is the whole result workflow. The live session showed otherwise. `My progress` gave an initial academic-history view, but the fuller academic record appeared only after moving through the Support portal.

That deeper page verified:

- a full grade table
- CGPA
- current class of degree
- total credit earned
- outstanding credit
- outstanding courses
- `Export as PDF`
- `Print`

So if you need more than a basic result-history screen, this is the route that matters most.

## The Exact Verified Path

The observed workflow was:

1. log into the NOUN student portal
2. open `My progress`
3. read the note that points to `Support` and `eTicketing`
4. open `Support`
5. locate `Drop a complaint (e-Ticketing)`
6. move to the Support login page
7. log into the Support portal
8. open `Result statement`
9. allow the system to open the ERP result page in a new tab

The verified Support login route was:

- `https://support.nou.edu.ng/login/`

The authenticated dashboard route observed was:

- `https://support.nou.edu.ng/user`

The final statement-of-result destination was:

- `https://erp.nou.edu.ng/stsor`

## What We Actually Observed After Support Login

The Support dashboard was not just a complaint inbox. It showed:

- ticket history
- student actions
- a dedicated `Result statement` action

That action is what makes this workflow valuable. It bridges the gap between the main student portal and the stronger ERP academic-record page.

This is why students searching only for a results button can get stuck. The best result surface is hidden behind a second authenticated step.

## Step-By-Step Guide

### Step 1: Start from the student portal result area

Open `My progress` first. The live portal itself told students to use `Support` and `eTicketing` to see more results.

If you need help finding that first page, read [How To Find NOUN Results On My Progress](/articles/how-to-find-noun-results-on-my-progress).

### Step 2: Use the Support route the portal recommends

Open `Support` from the portal. The observed support area included `Drop a complaint (e-Ticketing)` and result-related support categories such as `Registry`.

That means the support path is part of the real result workflow, not a random side tool.

### Step 3: Log into the Support portal

The verified login route was `https://support.nou.edu.ng/login/`. Use your own student access and keep control of your credentials.

Do not trust forwarded shortcuts that skip the official portal path. A clean support login is safer than chasing screenshots from other students.

### Step 4: Open Result statement

After login, look for `Result statement` in the student actions. In the observed session, the app triggered an internal function and opened the result page in a new tab.

The page that opened was the ERP statement interface, not the same page as `My progress`.

### Step 5: Review the ERP page carefully

Once the ERP page opens, do not only glance at the first visible block. The observed page had several academic sections:

- the full grade table
- summary statistics
- performance standing
- outstanding courses

## What This Page Does Not Prove

The verified workflow confirms how to reach the result statement. It does not prove:

- transcript requests
- carryover workflow labels
- result correction process

Those should not be added to your assumptions just because you reached the ERP page.

## Common Mistakes To Avoid

- stopping at `My progress` and never opening Support
- assuming Support is only for complaints and not for result access
- forgetting that the result page opens in a new tab
- using the ERP page without saving safe evidence
- confusing verified result-statement access with unverified transcript or correction workflows
