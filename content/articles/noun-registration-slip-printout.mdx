## Where to open the current registration slip

To print your NOUN registration slip:

1. Log into NOUN portal
2. Open the current course- or exam-registration record
3. Use the print, download, or save option the page provides
4. Check the session, course codes, and registration status on the saved copy
5. Keep it privately and follow the current exam instructions on whether a printed copy is required

---


## Which Slip You Need And What To Check

This guide explains where registration slips fit in the student workflow, what details to verify, and how to preserve a safe copy.


## What Are Registration Slips?

**Course Registration Slip:**
- Proof you're registered for courses
- Shows course codes, titles, credits
- Useful when you need to compare a later course or result record

**Exam Registration Slip:**
- Proof you're registered to sit exam
- May show exam details provided for the current session
- Bring it only in the form required by the current exam instructions

## Step-by-Step: Printing Registration Slip

### Step 1: Log In to Portal

1. Go to the official NOUN student portal
2. Log in with Student ID and password

### Step 2: Find Course Registration Section

1. Open the course-registration area shown on your dashboard
2. Look for the submitted registration record or slip option
3. Compare the visible list with the courses you intended to register

### Step 3: Save The Registration Slip

1. Use the print, download, or save option shown by the current page
2. Open the saved record
3. Confirm the student details, session, and course or exam entries
4. Give the file a private name that helps you identify the semester

### Step 4: Print the Slip

Option A: Print from browser
1. Open the PDF
2. Press **Ctrl+P** (or Cmd+P on Mac)
3. Select printer
4. Click **"Print"**

Option B: Save to computer first
1. Open PDF
2. File → Save As
3. Choose location on computer
4. Later: Find file → Right-click → Print

## When You Need Registration Slip

### Course Registration Slip
- Comparing course registration with exam registration or results
- Reporting a missing or incorrect course
- Keeping a semester record of what the portal accepted

### Exam Registration Slip
- Follow the current examination notice on whether to bring a printout, photocopy, or another record
- Check that the course, venue, mode, and session details match your instructions

## Troubleshooting

### Issue: "Print button not showing"

Try:
1. Refresh page (F5)
2. Clear browser cache
3. Try different browser
4. Contact support

### Issue: "Can't open PDF file"

Solution:
1. Download Adobe Reader (free)
2. Open PDF with Adobe Reader
3. Then print

### Issue: "The slip should exist but I cannot find it"

Return to the current official workflow and compare the registration state first. If the underlying course or exam record is incomplete, the printing problem may actually be a registration-state problem rather than a PDF problem. Use [How To Register NOUN Courses](/articles/how-to-register-noun-courses) or [NOUN Exam Registration Guide](/articles/noun-exam-registration-guide) to confirm the earlier step.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: Do I need original slip or photocopy?

Follow the current instructions for your examination centre. This article does not verify one document format for every centre.

### Q2: What if I lose my slip before exam?

Return to the current registration record and use the available save or print option. If the record is no longer available, ask the official support route how to retrieve it.

### Q3: Do I need slip for every course?

Check the course list on the slip you generated. Do not assume it includes an entry that is missing from the page.

## Important Warnings

⚠️ **Do NOT:**
- Leave home without registration slip on exam days
- Lose your only copy (print multiple copies)
- Rely solely on digital copy (examiners may require physical)
- Use outdated slip (reprint if you add/drop courses)

⚠️ **Critical:**
- Some exam centres **require physical registration slip** as proof of enrollment
- Without slip, you may be denied exam entry
- Updated slip is needed if you register additional courses mid-semester

✓ **DO:**
- Print at least 2 copies immediately after registration
- Store one in secure location
- Carry one when going to exam centre
- Reprint if you make any registration changes

---

## Save the current version of your slip

Save the slip after registration and read it before relying on it. The important details are the student record, session, course codes, and current status. If anything is missing, resolve the registration record rather than treating printing as the main problem.

## Requirements Before You Start

Open the submitted registration record for the correct session and check every code before saving the slip. Keep the file private because it can contain student details.

## Common Mistakes

- Using another student's slip as a format guide for your own record.
- Printing before checking the session and course or exam entries.
- Keeping only one unsaved browser copy.
- Sending the full slip and login details to a printing helper.
- Reprinting repeatedly when the underlying registration is wrong.

## Official Sources and Verification Notes

The slip records what the portal accepted at that point. If the course or exam list is wrong, correct the underlying registration rather than relying on another printout.

## Practical Student Workflow

A registration slip is proof of what the portal accepted at that time. Save it immediately after a successful registration, keep a backup copy, and compare the course and exam slips before examination preparation. If the slip does not load, document the issue before deadlines create pressure.

Save the current slip, check its session and entries, and follow the examination notice on whether to bring a printed or digital copy.
