# Final Authority Coverage Audit

Last updated: 2026-06-20

## Coverage Summary

NounCompass now covers all major NOUN student authority branches, but coverage depth is uneven. The site is strongest where it has a pillar plus several workflow-specific support pages. It is weakest where a topic is represented by one or two pages, generic explanation pages, or pages that are not well integrated into the wider student journey.

| Area | Coverage Status | Current Strength | Main Weaknesses |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Admissions | Strong | Good entry-stage guidance with application and requirements coverage | Missing admission-status, screening, offer-acceptance, and post-admission onboarding workflow |
| Registration | Strong | Excellent pillar coverage around course registration and related limits | Slip printout and missing-course-code support are lighter and not strongly surfaced |
| Fees | Strong | Deepest article count and strongest payment-support spine | Several older fee pages still read more generically than the newer evidence-based clusters |
| Portal | Moderate | Dashboard, password, profile, and course-task pages exist | Portal-specific issue coverage is still narrower than registration coverage |
| Course Materials | Weak | Course-material library exists and one article supports it | Very little supporting editorial depth and weak contextual linking |
| TMA/eLearn | Strong | Pillar plus multiple support pages with real workflow grounding | Could still use stronger exam and support crossover links |
| Examinations | Moderate | Exam registration pillar is strong | Only two exam-focused articles, with one effectively orphaned |
| Results | Strong | Good research-based results pillar and supporting pages | Several support pages are under 1,000 words and rely on the pillar for depth |
| Support | Moderate | One strong support-ticket guide with sitewide reuse | Too dependent on a single article rather than a broader support cluster |
| Study Centres | Strong | National pillar plus state pages and verification-first framing | Student task guidance inside location pages is still more repetitive than workflow-specific |
| NELFUND | Strong | Full pillar-and-support cluster built from verified evidence | Edge-case eligibility and later-stage process details remain intentionally unresolved |
| Student Workflows | Moderate | Graduation-cost and cross-cluster guides provide some lifecycle coverage | Onboarding, deferment, reactivation, and graduation-path workflows are still thin |

## Missing Workflows

These are the most important workflow gaps left after the cluster build phase:

- admission status checking and what to do after submitting an application
- screening, clearance, or offer-acceptance path after admission
- stronger portal issue workflows beyond password, profile, and course-code basics
- exam timetable / venue / exam-day evidence workflow beyond registration
- course-materials usage workflow beyond one PDF-vs-hardcopy explainer
- support escalation variants for non-results and non-fee issues
- deferment, study break, reactivation, or academic-resumption guidance

## Missing Explanations

The biggest explanatory gaps are not total absences anymore. They are missing second-layer answers:

- what happens after admission, not just how to apply
- what happens after course registration, not just how to submit it
- what happens after exam registration, not just how to verify it
- how course materials fit into results, GST, and study-planning journeys
- how support should differ by finance, registration, result, and study-centre issues

## Missing Student Questions

These question groups still look under-served:

- `How do I know what happens after NOUN admission submission?`
- `How do I check whether my admission was accepted or delayed?`
- `What should I bring or verify before an exam day?`
- `How do I escalate a portal issue that is not password reset or course-code related?`
- `How do I use course materials effectively after downloading them?`
- `What should I do if I need to pause and resume my academic progress later?`

## Weak Topic Areas

The weakest topical areas right now are:

- Course Materials
- Student Workflows beyond fee / registration / result tasks
- Examinations beyond registration
- Support as a standalone authority branch
- Portal issue resolution beyond the core registration flow

## Coverage Verdict

NounCompass is no longer missing major authority branches. The remaining coverage problem is uneven depth, not missing intent alignment. That means the site is ready to move into Phase 8, but the authority audit still leaves a short list of content-system weaknesses that should be corrected before launch-quality signoff.
