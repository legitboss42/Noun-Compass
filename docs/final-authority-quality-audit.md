# Final Authority Quality Audit

Last updated: 2026-06-20

## Quality Summary

The newer research-led clusters are materially stronger than many earlier fee, portal, and explainer pages. The site now has two different content standards sitting next to each other:

- newer workflow-grounded pages with stronger verification boundaries
- older listicle-style pages that are still useful, but less precise and less authoritative

## Thin Content Findings

The thinnest pages relative to cluster importance are:

- `how-to-check-outstanding-courses-on-noun-result-statement`
- `noun-registration-slip-printout`
- `noun-school-fees-returning-students`
- `noun-school-fees-new-students`
- `noun-financial-statement`
- `how-to-use-noun-support-or-e-ticketing-for-result-problems`

These are not empty pages, but they are lighter than the authority role they are currently being asked to play.

## Duplicate Or Repetitive Content Findings

The site no longer has the earlier duplicate-section problem in the article body set, but there is still repetitive structural wording across older content:

- repeated generic `Requirements Before You Start` blocks
- repeated generic `Common Mistakes` and `Official Sources and Verification Notes` sections
- repeated study-centre caution language across state pages

This is mostly a quality and differentiation issue, not a direct duplication penalty risk.

## Outdated Assumptions

The most significant outdated or weakly supported assumptions are clustered in older finance and portal articles:

- departmental installment exceptions presented too confidently
- informal hardship and scholarship routes presented without precise sourcing
- guessed portal labels or button names in older pages
- broad academic examples used where programme-specific verification would be safer

## Unsupported-Claim Risk

The site's biggest unsupported-claim risk is now localized rather than systemic. The newer NELFUND pages are disciplined. The older finance pages are where stronger-than-evidence wording is still most likely to appear.

## Generic AI-Style Writing Findings

The pages most likely to feel generic rather than workflow-grounded are:

- `noun-installment-payment`
- `noun-registration-slip-printout`
- `noun-core-courses-vs-electives`
- `noun-e-exam-vs-pop`

They are not unreadable, but they do not create the same `this was written from real NOUN familiarity` feeling as the newer results, TMA, study-centre, and NELFUND pages.

## FAQ And Troubleshooting Findings

Sitewide FAQ coverage is good because the template and FAQ registry are consistent. The weaker issue is FAQ specificity:

- older articles often use generic preventive answers
- some newer NELFUND pages rely on article-level routing rather than explicit FAQ headings
- support pages sometimes need one more layer of task-specific troubleshooting

## Quality Verdict

The authority system is good enough to move into Phase 8, but not clean enough to declare a content-quality finish. The site now needs selective refinement, not broad rewriting. The most important fix is to raise the older fee and portal pages up to the stricter standard already visible in the newer clusters.
