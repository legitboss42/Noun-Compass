## Intent Buckets

### 1. Eligibility intent

Students want to know whether they should even invest time in the application.

Best future angle:
- focus on what is officially confirmed for NOUN
- separate confirmed facts from unresolved category questions

### 2. Workflow intent

Students want the exact order of actions, especially where:

- institution selection appears
- matric number is entered
- JAMB verification happens

Best future angle:
- explain only the verified public flow
- avoid inventing unobserved post-verification steps

### 3. Troubleshooting intent

Students need help when the system blocks them or their data does not align.

Best future angle:
- use the official `missing information` and JAMB-verification requirements
- explain possible mismatch sources carefully without claiming hidden portal rules

### 4. Disbursement intent

Students want to know what happens after approval.

Best future angle:
- use the verified NOUN e-wallet announcement
- avoid publishing unsupported timing promises

## Recommended SERP Strategy

The NELFUND cluster should target searchers in this order:

1. `Can I use this at NOUN?`
2. `How does the official flow work?`
3. `Why is the portal blocking me?`
4. `What happens after approval?`

That order matches the verified user journey better than jumping straight into general student-loan education.

## Query Types To Treat Carefully

These likely have demand, but they need stronger evidence before being answered aggressively:

- `NELFUND for NOUN postgraduate students`
- `NELFUND regularization deadline for NOUN`
- `how long NELFUND takes to reflect on NOUN e-wallet`
- `how NOUN students correct NELFUND rejection`
