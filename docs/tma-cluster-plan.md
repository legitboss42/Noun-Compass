# NOUN eLearn + TMA Cluster Plan

Captured: 2026-06-19

Goal: design the full eLearn/TMA authority cluster from observed platform reality, without creating content in this phase.

## Executive Direction

NounCompass already has decent coverage for:

- fees
- registration
- portal account tasks
- results
- support
- GST
- course materials

The missing authority layer is the actual learning workflow inside eLearn, especially how students move from course access to TMA completion and score verification.

## Student Journey Structure

Observed journey NounCompass should support:

1. Student gains admission.
2. Student pays fees.
3. Student registers semester and courses.
4. Student enters eLearn.
5. Student finds course page.
6. Student finds TMA section.
7. Student understands attempt rules and deadline.
8. Student submits TMA.
9. Student checks TMA score.
10. Student escalates when the workflow breaks.

The TMA cluster should connect the middle of this journey, not stand alone.

| Escalation page | Tell students when to use official support |
| Supporting ecosystem links | Registration, results, GST, support, course materials |

## Evidence-Based Notes

- The observed course used a dedicated `TMA` section with `TMA1`, `TMA2`, and `TMA3`.
- The observed TMA page displayed open/close dates and a one-attempt rule.
- The observed course grade report displayed TMA weights, scores, and course total.
- The observed session showed that students can enter the workflow from the dashboard, the course page, or the grade area.

## What Not To Do

- Do not publish generic Moodle advice that is not tied to the NOUN interface.
- Do not assume every course has the same visual structure without further checks.
- Do not treat results content and eLearn/TMA content as the same cluster.
- Do not create TMA articles that skip screenshots or skip the real workflow labels students actually see.
