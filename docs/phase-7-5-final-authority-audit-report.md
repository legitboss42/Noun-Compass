# Phase 7.5 Final Authority Audit Report

Last updated: 2026-06-20

## Executive Summary

NounCompass is now a real NOUN topical authority site, not just a useful resource. The major authority branches exist, the internal linking backbone is mostly coherent, and the newer clusters show a strong verification-first editorial standard.

The audit did not find a missing-cluster problem anymore. It found a consistency problem:

- a small group of older articles still operate below the newer authority standard
- 8 pages are contextually orphaned
- source specificity is weaker than it should be on many older pages
- one older finance page remains the clearest authority risk

- 8 orphaned pages weaken the content ecosystem

## AdSense Blockers

No hard authority-based AdSense blocker was found at the sitewide level.

The closest AdSense-quality risks from this phase are:

- one high-risk finance article with weaker claim discipline
- uneven quality between newer and older clusters
- generic source references on older pages

## Remaining Trust Gaps

- homepage-level or broad portal URLs used where more precise official sources should exist
- inconsistent in-body verification and last-reviewed sections across older articles
- weaker first-hand or observed-workflow feel on older listicle-style pages
- orphan pages that reduce the appearance of editorial system quality

## Recommended Fixes

- tighten `noun-installment-payment`
- recover the orphan pages through contextual linking
- improve thin support pages tied to results, fees, and portal tasks
- raise source specificity across older content
- strengthen Course Materials and Support as clusters, not just individual pages

## Go / No-Go For Phase 8

**Go for Phase 8**

Reason:

- the major authority branches are in place
- the site builds cleanly
- the remaining issues are important but selective, not structural blockers
- Phase 8 metadata and schema work can proceed while the authority remediation list is queued for cleanup before launch signoff

## Final Verdict

NounCompass is ready to move from authority building into launch-preparation work, but not ready to treat authority work as finished. The site has crossed the threshold into real topical authority. The next step is to preserve that gain by aligning the older archive with the newer evidence-first standard.
