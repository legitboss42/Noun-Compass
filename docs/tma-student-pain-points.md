# NOUN eLearn TMA Student Pain Points

Captured: 2026-06-19

This list is based on real interface observation, not generic LMS assumptions.

## Highest-Value Pain Points

| Pain Point | What We Observed | Why Students May Search It | Likely Search Intent |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cannot find TMA inside eLearn | TMAs are nested under a long course page and a separate `TMA` section | Students may see the course but still miss the TMA block | navigational + how-to |
| My courses page feels unclear | `My courses` showed controls, but the session did not clearly surface the course cards/list students would expect | Students may think their courses are missing | troubleshooting |
| TMA attempt rules are easy to miss | The page shows `Attempts allowed: 1`, but that detail is mixed into the activity page | Students may start without realizing there is only one try | warning + prevention |
| Closed TMA confusion | Open and closed timestamps are visible, but students may only notice them after the deadline | Missed deadlines create urgent search behavior | troubleshooting + deadline clarity |
| Finished attempt but no review | The observed summary said `Review not permitted` | Students may expect to re-open answers or confirm what they submitted | troubleshooting |
| Score location is not obvious | Scores appear on the grade report, not directly as a simple final message on the TMA page | Students may not know whether to check the TMA page, course page, or grades page | navigational + troubleshooting |
| Multiple entry paths create ambiguity | Dashboard shortcuts, course page, course index, and grades all expose different pieces of the same workflow | Students may not know the correct place to start | how-to |

## Mistake-Prone Steps

1. Entering the course page and stopping at the first visible sections instead of scrolling to `TMA`.
2. Assuming `My courses` will always be the clearest place to find activities.
3. Ignoring `Opened` and `Closed` timestamps until the deadline has passed.
4. Expecting multiple tries when the activity allows only one.
5. Looking for TMA marks only on the TMA page instead of checking `Grades`.

## Content Implications

The weak spot is not a lack of topics. The weak spot is workflow clarity.

The future cluster should focus on:

- where to find TMAs
- what the TMA page means
- deadline and attempt rules
- where scores appear
- what to do when a course or TMA does not show
- when to escalate to support

## Priority Order

| Priority | Topic Direction | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Finding TMAs and understanding the TMA page | Highest navigational confusion and strongest search intent |
| 2 | Submission rules, deadline checks, and one-attempt warnings | Prevents costly student mistakes |
| 3 | Missing TMA, missing course, or hidden navigation troubleshooting | Strong support-value content |
| 4 | Where scores appear after submission | Strong post-submission demand |
| 5 | Support escalation guide for eLearn-specific issues | Helpful when self-service fails |
