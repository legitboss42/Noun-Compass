# NOUN Results Student Pain Points

Last updated: 2026-06-19

## Scope

These pain points are based only on directly observed portal behavior during the live session. They are not inferred from generic LMS patterns.

## Observed Pain Points

| Pain Point | What Was Observed | Why It Matters | Likely Search Intent |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Results entry point is not labeled as `Results` | the portal used `My progress` as the primary results page label | students expecting a clear `Results` menu may not know where to click | `how to check noun result`, `where is result on noun portal` |
| Result page showed grade columns but visible rows contained dashes | `Grade` existed as a column, but observed rows displayed `-` | students may assume results are missing, unreleased, or broken | `noun result not showing`, `noun grade dash meaning` |
| No visible semester selector on the results page | historical periods were listed inline as `2024_1` and `2024_2`, with no dropdown filter | students may struggle to understand whether all semesters are loaded automatically or whether current results are missing | `how to select semester on noun result portal` |
| Results page explicitly pushes students to Support/e-Ticketing | visible note said `Click on Support (top right), eTicketing (Left) and login to see more results` | students may need a second workflow to resolve missing details | `how to complain about noun result`, `noun result complaint portal` |
| Support login is a separate workflow from the student portal | student must leave the portal, log into support, and then use `Result statement` | this adds friction and makes the strongest result page harder to discover | `noun support portal login`, `result statement on noun support portal` |
| Registration-history evidence can be blocked | `See course registration slip` returned a blocker asking the student to register the current semester first | students trying to verify missing results may hit a dead end before they can access academic proof | `noun course registration slip not showing`, `please register for current semester noun` |
| Full academic metrics live on a different page than `My progress` | CGPA, class of degree, failed courses, and outstanding credit were only verified on the ERP statement-of-result page | students can stop too early if they only use `My progress` | `how to check noun cgpa`, `how to see noun statement of result` |
| Outstanding courses are visible only on the deeper result page | an `Outstanding Courses` section appeared on the ERP page, not on the first student-portal result view | students may not realize the system already lists unfinished requirements | `noun outstanding courses`, `how to check carryover on noun portal` |

## Issues Explicitly Not Confirmed

The following common student issues were not directly observable in this session, so they should not yet be treated as verified content angles:

- transcript request workflow
- statement of result workflow
- carryover list workflow
- outstanding courses workflow
- in-portal result correction form

## Strongest Verified Search Opportunities

Based on the live session, the strongest verified angles are:

1. `How to check NOUN results on My progress`
2. `How to use NOUN support portal Result statement`
3. `Why NOUN result grade is not showing`
4. `What to do when NOUN asks you to use Support/e-Ticketing for more results`
5. `Why NOUN course registration slip says register for current semester first`
6. `How to read NOUN CGPA and outstanding credit`

## Student-Frustration Summary

The observed workflow creates confusion in three main ways:

- the portal uses `My progress` instead of an obvious `Results` label
- the grade structure is visible even when the actual grade cells do not show resolved values
- support escalation is baked into the workflow, which means students may need to cross from the student portal into the support portal and then into the ERP result page
