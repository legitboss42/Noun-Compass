# Phase 7 Topical Authority Report

## 1. Executive Summary

NounCompass is already a focused NOUN student resource, not a random collection of blog posts. The site has 30 tightly aligned published articles, task-based category hubs, a fee checker, a course-materials library, trust pages, author pages, and consistent NOUN-specific internal linking.

The problem is completeness, not relevance. Fees and portal/registration are well developed. Admission is solid. But TMA, NELFUND, study-centre depth, and academic-record depth are too thin or missing. That keeps the site from functioning as a full topical authority ecosystem across all of the stated NOUN student needs.

**Current verdict:** `B. A useful student resource`

**Topical Authority Score:** `62/100`

## 2. Cluster Analysis

See the full table in [phase-7-cluster-map.md](/C:/Users/vicky/Desktop/Web%20Growth/NounCompass/docs/phase-7-cluster-map.md).

Top findings:

- Fees & E-Wallet is the strongest cluster because it combines 12 articles with the `/fees` tool and multiple payment-support spokes.
- Registration & Portal is the second-strongest cluster because `how-to-register-noun-courses` is supported by nine closely related workflow pages.
- Admission has a real mini-cluster with qualification and application support, but it stops short of status-checking and onboarding completion.
- Results, Study Centres, Student Support, GST, and Course Materials each rely on one key page or one strong section surface, which is useful but not authority-grade depth.
- NELFUND is missing entirely as a cluster.

### Google Understanding Test

**Likely answer:** `B. Student resource website`

Why:

- The navigation, category hubs, and article set are all tightly focused on NOUN student tasks.
- The site has trust pages, author pages, FAQ and HowTo structure, fee and course-material tools, and consistent student-workflow language.
- The internal linking now clearly connects many operational journeys.
- But Google would still see incomplete coverage in several expected authority branches: NELFUND, TMA, academic records, and national study centres.

That makes the site more than a loose article collection, but not yet a complete topical authority website.

## 7. Safe Improvements Implemented

Implemented during this audit:

- Fixed `55` stale relative article links so they now resolve to valid `/articles/...` routes.
- Added `10` new contextual hub or pillar links to strengthen weak ecosystem paths.
- Improved portal-support discovery by linking the dashboard and support-oriented articles more directly into registration, exams, results, and course materials.
- Added direct cluster references from weak nodes into `/portal`, `/course-materials`, and `/examinations` where appropriate.
- Verified with a local resolver that all article-body internal links now point to valid known routes.

## 8. Remaining Authority Weaknesses

- No NELFUND cluster at all.
- No TMA / eLearn content even though TMA is one of the intended authority topics.
- Results authority is concentrated in one article.
- Study-centre authority is concentrated in one Lagos-only article.
- Course-material authority depends heavily on the `/course-materials` section and lacks article-layer depth.
- Several utility pages in the portal cluster still depend on generic template-like related-guide blocks instead of richer contextual linking.

## Final Classification

**Is NounCompass currently:**

- `A. A collection of articles` — No
- `B. A useful student resource` — Yes
- `C. A topical authority website` — Not yet

Evidence for `B`:

- 30 articles all focused on NOUN student workflows
- category hubs by task
- fee checker and course-materials library
- trust, policy, and author pages
- strong NOUN-specific internal linking and schema support

Evidence against `C`:

- no NELFUND cluster
- no TMA cluster
- very thin results and study-centre depth
- no national study-centre authority surface
- no academic-records depth beyond result checking
