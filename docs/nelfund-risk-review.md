# NELFUND Risk Review

Last updated: 2026-06-20

## Why This Cluster Needs Extra Caution

NELFUND is a student-finance topic. That makes accuracy, update discipline, and wording control more important than with ordinary informational content.

## Risk Summary

| Risk | Severity | Why It Matters | Recommended Control |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Policy drift | Critical | eligibility and workflow rules can change quickly | date every NELFUND article and re-verify against official sources before major updates |
| Category-specific eligibility overclaiming | Critical | students may rely on incorrect statements about new, returning, or postgraduate eligibility | separate `verified`, `partially verified`, and `unverified` claims in all future content |
| JAMB regularization overstatement | Critical | students may spend money or time based on a rule that was not directly confirmed | do not present regularization as a universal requirement unless an official source states it clearly |
| Portal workflow drift | High | the public application flow may change without notice | re-check the official registration flow before publishing or refreshing workflow content |
| Financial expectation mismatch | High | students may expect immediate fee reflection or approval timing | avoid timing promises and use cautious language around disbursement and reflection |
| Incomplete NOUN-side visibility | High | the official NOUN disbursement post proves outcomes, but not every intermediate step | do not invent internal NOUN processing steps |
| Official-source scarcity on edge cases | High | public official pages did not clearly answer every common student question | explicitly mark unresolved items rather than filling gaps with assumption |
| Existing NounCompass content drift | Medium | older site content may already contain broad NELFUND statements that now need stricter sourcing | review and tighten older NELFUND mentions before scaling this cluster |

## Official-Source Limitations Seen In This Pass

The following limitations were observed during research:

- the public NELFUND site exposed the registration workflow more clearly than its FAQ content
- the reviewed official sources did not clearly settle postgraduate, returning-student, or new-student eligibility
- the exact NOUN-side record-synchronization path was not documented in a clear public official guide

## Safe Publishing Rules For Future NELFUND Content

Every future NELFUND article should:

- link to the official NOUN or official NELFUND source behind each major claim
- state when a point is verified, partially verified, or unverified
- avoid promises about approval speed, disbursement timing, or automatic eligibility
- avoid publishing portal-specific steps that were not directly observed
- avoid using old forum, WhatsApp, or social-media claims as authority

## Recommended Refresh Cadence

Because this topic can change quickly, the safest editorial standard is:

- re-check official sources before publishing the first cluster article
- re-check again before any later major refresh
- re-check immediately if NOUN or NELFUND publishes a new eligibility notice, FAQ, or application update

## Bottom-Line Risk Judgment

This cluster is worth building because:

- the student value is high
- the authority gap is currently large
- official NOUN participation is now strongly supported

But it should be built as a controlled authority cluster, not a high-volume content sprint.
