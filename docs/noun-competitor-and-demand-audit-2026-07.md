# NOUN Competitor and Student-Demand Audit

Reviewed: 2026-07-19

## Method and limits

The review combined official NOUN surfaces with discoverable independent platforms returned for query clusters covering NOUN course materials, past questions, TMA practice, result and fee checkers, exam timetables, study planning, accounts, dashboards, and student communities. Domains were deduplicated and features were recorded only when visible on a live, indexed page.

The web is unbounded and changes continuously. This is a broad discoverable-market audit, not a claim that every NOUN-related page on the internet has been found.

## Demand inference

Timetables, exam practice, course resources, fee planning, and progress dashboards recur across otherwise different competitors. That repetition is a strong product-demand proxy, although it is not equivalent to verified keyword volume. NounCompass's July analytics also showed that practical fee, Remita, portal, CGPA, and guide surfaces attracted the strongest early engagement.

## Chosen product gap

The audited set did not reveal one privacy-first workflow that combines selected courses, official materials, fee and CGPA tools, weekly study planning, versioned exam schedules, preparation readiness, source confidence, membership, and support without asking for NOUN credentials. NounCompass will use that combined semester command centre as its differentiator.

## Exclusions

- No leaked examination content or active TMA answers.
- No runtime paid AI dependency in v1.
- No student uploads, public forum, admission documents, or community moderation in v1.
- No claim that unofficial fee, calendar, or schedule information is official without a recorded source and review.
